# Demand & exports for the high-margin food-processing products — and the Mega Food Park fit

*Market-demand and export evaluation of the high-margin products ranked in
[product-margins-ranked.md](product-margins-ranked.md), tied to India's Mega Food Park
infrastructure. This report supplies the **demand and export** column the margin ranking
lacked; it does **not** repeat the DPR margins ([product-margins-ranked.md](product-margins-ranked.md)),
the import-substitution savings arithmetic ([consolidated-savings-by-crop.md](consolidated-savings-by-crop.md)),
or the full product map ([byproduct-product-tree.md](byproduct-product-tree.md)). Data:
[`data/product_demand_exports.csv`](../data/product_demand_exports.csv). Every figure is labelled
**verified** (official/trade — APEDA, DGCI&S, PIB, Spices Board, company filings), **reported**
(private market-research house), or **estimated** (analyst inference). July 2026.*

---

## Why this report exists

The margin ranking answered *"which product earns the best spread per kg."* It did not answer
*"who buys it, how big is the market, and does it export."* A fat DPR margin on a product with no
demand is a trap; a thin margin on a product with captive government demand or a proven export book
is bankable. This report pairs each high-margin product with its **domestic market size + CAGR,
export volume/value + destinations, price realisation, and demand outlook** — then sorts the whole
set into a **demand-tier map** and names the products whose margin + demand + export profiles
actually coincide.

## Per-product demand & export

### 1. Rice bran oil (RBO) — the volume anchor, both import-substitution *and* a real export
- **Domestic:** India is the **world's #1 RBO market (36.7% share, 2024)** and produces **~60% of
  global refined RBO**. India's RBO market ~**USD 1.17 bn (2019) → ~1.39 bn (2024), ~3% CAGR**
  (*reported*; global-market CAGR estimates span a wide 1.7–11.1% by house).
- **Export:** India *does* export RBO — **~USD 104.5 mn (2024, ~4.36% of global RBO export value)**
  (*reported*, Tridge). Top destinations **Netherlands, Japan, USA, Italy**; recent 6-month flow
  **USA, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, Vietnam, Australia**. Realisation **USD 0.45–2.47/kg**.
- **Demand outlook:** The health-oil positioning (oryzanol, 232 °C smoke point, cholesterol-lowering)
  plus NAFED's 2021 fortified-RBO launch under *Aatmanirbhar Bharat* make this **both** the
  ~₹7,544 cr import-substitution anchor ([consolidated-savings-by-crop.md](consolidated-savings-by-crop.md))
  **and** a modest but genuine export line — the deepest, most price-inelastic offtake in the tree.
  [imarcgroup.com](https://www.imarcgroup.com/rice-bran-oil-processing-plant) ·
  [tridge.com](https://www.tridge.com/tridge-woods/rice-bran-oil/IN) ·
  [mordorintelligence.com](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/rice-bran-oil-market)

### 2. Basmati rice — the proven high-value rice export *(verified, APEDA)*
- **Export:** **60.65 LMT in 2024-25, worth ~₹50,000 cr (~USD 5.94 bn)** — up 8.23 LMT (+15.7% vol)
  and ~₹1,923 cr over 2023-24. Realisation ~**₹82/kg** (value ÷ volume), the highest of any rice line.
- **Top destinations:** **Saudi Arabia** (11.73 LMT / ₹10,190 cr), **Iraq** (9.05 LMT / ₹7,201 cr),
  **Iran** (8.55 LMT), then Yemen, UAE, USA, UK, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar — **154 countries**.
- **Demand outlook:** The KRBL / LT Foods branded-export model proven at scale; **Iran
  payment/geopolitics is the standing risk** to a ~₹6,000 cr slice. This is the single clearest
  "process-and-brand pays" export in the whole book.
  [apeda.gov.in/BasmatiRice](https://apeda.gov.in/BasmatiRice) ·
  [ricenewstoday.com](https://ricenewstoday.com/indian-basmati-24-25-exports-up-rs1923cr-over-last-season/)

### 3. Non-basmati rice — volume, not value *(verified, APEDA)*
- **Export:** **14.13 mn MT, ₹55,408 cr / USD 6,527.64 mn (2024-25 APEDA)** — realisation only
  ~**₹39/kg** (commodity). Top markets **Benin, Guinea, Côte d'Ivoire, Togo, Bangladesh** (~44% of
  volume). Included for contrast: it moves the tonnage but captures none of the branded premium —
  the opposite of basmati.
  [apeda.gov.in/NonBasmatiRice](https://apeda.gov.in/NonBasmatiRice)

### 4. Poha / murmura / value-added rice FMCG — the top DPR-margin product, real diaspora export
- **Domestic:** Poha/murmura ride India's **packaged-rice market 13.68 MT (2025) → 21.09 MT (2034),
  4.75% CAGR** and the **Indian ethnic-snacks market USD 5.18 bn (2024) → 17.5 bn (2033), 14.5% CAGR**
  (*reported*).
- **Export:** Carried inside India's **snack exports of USD 1.2 bn (2024), up from 0.54 bn (2019) —
  ~17% CAGR**; **USA takes 23.9%** (4.5 mn-strong diaspora), then UK, Canada, Australia, Gulf.
  Poha-specific volume isn't broken out, but it is explicitly named as a growth ethnic line.
- **Demand outlook:** The NIFTEM-T DPR's **10–15% net margin** (the ranking's standout) meets **both**
  strong domestic FMCG breakfast demand **and** a fast-growing diaspora ethnic-food pull — a rare
  margin-plus-demand-plus-export triple on a ₹21-lakh capex.
  [imarcgroup.com](https://www.imarcgroup.com/india-packaged-rice-market) ·
  [naviexports.com](https://www.naviexports.com/blog/exporting-indian-snacks-sweets-the-global-desi-food-boom/)

### 5. Maize starch + derivatives (HFCS, sorbitol, modified starch) — the wet-milling industry
- **Domestic:** **India corn-starch USD 3.78 bn (2023) → 4.76 bn (2030), 5.2% CAGR**; broader
  **starch & derivatives USD 3.45 bn (2025) → 4.12 bn (2030), 7.34% CAGR**; **modified starch
  USD 536.5 mn (2025) → 804.7 mn (2034), 4.47% CAGR** (*reported*). India is the **world's #3
  producer** (output 2.6 mn t vs consumption 2 mn t → net exporter), yet only ~14% of maize is
  wet-milled and it still **imports high-purity derivatives**.
- **Export:** Net exporter of starch; **corn gluten meal already ships to 25+ countries**. The
  pharma-excipient segment is the fastest sub-market (**8.8% CAGR**).
- **Demand outlook:** Real domestic-commercial growth; the capex (₹80–120 cr) is the barrier, not the
  demand — the "modern integrated plant" white space.
  [industryarc.com](https://www.industryarc.com/Report/18431/india-corn-starch-market-research-report-Market-Research) ·
  [mordorintelligence.com](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/india-starch-and-starch-derivative-market)

### 6. Vital wheat gluten — import-substitution on wheat India already holds *(verified import data)*
- **Domestic:** A **niche** in India (displaced by soya/legumes today), but bakery + meat-analogue +
  aquafeed-binder demand is the emerging driver; **global VWG USD 2.8 bn (2024) → 4.2 bn (2033),
  4.6% CAGR**.
- **Import-substitution:** India **imports ~13,000 t/yr, China ~88%/largest supplier**, at
  **~USD 1,292/t (~₹108/kg)** — a ~₹140 cr import bill on a 75%-protein product made by wet-milling
  the wheat the state stockpiles. Not an export play (yet); a classic "make the derivative, stop
  importing it" case.
  [indexbox.io](https://www.indexbox.io/store/india-wheat-gluten-market-analysis-forecast-size-trends-and-insights/) ·
  [market.us](https://market.us/report/wheat-gluten-market/)

### 7. Pulse / pea / soy protein isolate — the global plant-protein boom
- **Domestic:** **India plant-based-protein USD 779.34 mn (2025) → 1,435.69 mn (2035), 6.3% CAGR**;
  **pea-protein isolate = 50.78% of the India market**.
- **Global demand:** **Pea-protein isolate USD 2.68 bn (2024) → 6.74 bn (2033), 10.8% CAGR**; whole
  **plant-based-protein USD 27.48 bn (2025) → 78.94 bn (2033), 14.1% CAGR** — the fastest-growing
  demand in this report.
- **Export:** India already ships to **US/UK/EU/UAE** with an estimated **~9.4% export CAGR through
  2034**; MoFPI has published a dedicated **plant-based-protein sector profile**. India's pulse base
  makes it a natural export-source candidate — the ">60% conversion margin" from the byproduct tree
  meets a genuinely booming global market.
  [mordorintelligence.com](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/india-pea-protein-market) ·
  [fortunebusinessinsights.com](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/pea-protein-market-106053) ·
  [mofpi.gov.in sector profile](https://mofpi.gov.in/sites/default/files/KnowledgeCentre/Sector%20Profile/Plant_based_Protein_sector_profile%20(1).pdf)

### 8. Nutraceuticals — gamma oryzanol / tocotrienols / natural vitamin E *(the irony line)*
- **Global demand:** **Gamma oryzanol USD 2.15 bn (2023), 3.6% CAGR to 2030**; **natural vitamin E
  USD 1.10 bn (2025) → 2.16 bn (2034), 7.78% CAGR**; **tocopherol USD 1.38 bn (2024) → 2.94 bn
  (2033), 8.75% CAGR**.
- **The gap:** India makes the RBO but **imports the isolate** — India is the **world's #1
  gamma-oryzanol *importer*** (111 shipments; sourced from US/Japan/Germany) and imports **60–70% of
  its natural vitamin E**, while its domestic oryzanol market is a trivial **~USD 6.5 mn**. TRF sells
  **USD 150–300/kg**, d-alpha tocopherol **USD 55–110/kg** — from a bran deodoriser-distillate
  feedstock at ₹45–75/kg. **India could be a natural-vitamin-E *export* source from its own bran.**
- **Demand outlook:** Highest ₹/kg in the book, but tonnage is tiny — an **export/high-value garnish**,
  not scalable volume (consistent with [byproduct-product-tree.md](byproduct-product-tree.md)).
  [grandviewresearch.com](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/gamma-oryzanol-market) ·
  [fortunebusinessinsights.com](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/industry-reports/natural-vitamin-e-market-101591) ·
  [volza.com](https://www.volza.com/p/gamma-oryzanol/import/import-in-india/)

### 9. Precipitated silica from rice-husk ash (green silica) — tyre-industry demand
- **Market:** **Global RHA-precipitated-silica USD 552 mn (2025) → 1,010 mn (2034), 10.4% CAGR** —
  and the **tyre industry is ~two-thirds of rice-husk-silica consumption** (rolling-resistance
  filler for green tyres).
- **India momentum (verified corporate signals):** **Tata Chemicals approved ₹775 cr (Nov 2025)** to
  add **50 ktpa** at Cuddalore (on 13.8 ktpa existing); the **world's first commercial-scale
  green-silica plant** (Goodyear + IISc, **USD 28 mn** FMO/DEG funding) is being built **in India**.
  Raw RHA ₹1–3.5/kg → silica = **~30× value jump**. India's tyre sector is the captive domestic
  buyer plus an export/import-substitution angle.
  [intelmarketresearch.com](https://www.intelmarketresearch.com/global-precipitated-silica-from-rice-husk-ash-forecast-market-26995) ·
  [entrepreneurindia.co](https://www.entrepreneurindia.co/blogs/rice-husk-silica-for-green-tyres/) ·
  [persistencemarketresearch.com](https://www.persistencemarketresearch.com/market-research/india-precipitated-silica-market.asp)

### 10. Besan / gram flour — domestic staple + diaspora export
- **Domestic:** India produces **>5 mn t besan**; **global gram-flour market USD 2.5 bn (2024) →
  4.1 bn (2033)**.
- **Export:** 48 countries; **USA + Australia + Singapore = ~70%** of besan exports (Oct-23→Sep-24).
  Thin DPR margin (1.5–3.2% net) but ~20% IRR; the state procures chana and sells it raw, capturing
  none of this.
  [verifiedmarketreports.com](https://www.verifiedmarketreports.com/product/gram-flour-market/) ·
  [volza.com](https://www.volza.com/p/besan-flour/export/export-from-india/)

### 11. Spices (value-added) — the record export line *(verified, Spices Board)*
- **Export:** **FY25 all-time high — 17.99 lakh t, ₹39,994.48 cr (USD 4,722.65 mn).** Value-addition
  (blends, oleoresins) is the APEDA/Spices Board priority. Not a grain-tree product, but the clearest
  proof that Indian processed-food value-addition exports at scale.
  [indianspices.com](https://www.indianspices.com/export/major-itemwise-export.html)

### 12. Fortified rice kernel (FRK) — the captive government demand, quantified *(verified)*
- **Captive demand:** FRK is blended **10 g/kg (1%)** into PDS rice (iron, folic acid, B12). At NFSA
  rice throughput of ~350–400 LMT, the 1% blend implies a **standing ~3.5–4 LMT/yr FRK demand the
  government itself creates and buys** — the surest demand in the whole book.
- **Scale (verified):** **~925 FRK manufacturers, 111 LMT/yr FRK-equivalent capacity; ~21,000 mills
  fitted with blenders (223 LMT/month fortified-rice blending capacity); 406 LMT fortified rice
  distributed 2019-20 → Mar 2024** (universal coverage reached Mar 2024). Program **₹17,082 cr
  allocated Jul 2024 → Dec 2028 (~₹2,700 cr/yr), 100% GoI-funded.**
- **Outlook:** Policy-set price (tender-competed), so the *margin* is modest and estimated (~8–15%);
  the value is the **guaranteed volume**. Fortified atta could become the same captive line if
  mandated.
  [pib.gov.in (PRID 2063447)](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2063447) ·
  [business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/agriculture/centre-approves-fortified-rice-supply-continuation-till-december-2028-124100900684_1.html)

### 13. Oilmeal / DORB — the export floor, already monetised *(verified)*
- **Export:** Record **48.86 LMT oilmeal in FY24, ₹15,368 cr (~USD 1.84 bn)**. De-oiled rice bran
  (DORB) export fell to **1.52 LMT (FY24)** under the July-2023 ban (reopened Oct-2025). Already
  monetised at commodity value; the SEA aqua-feed demand-creation gap is the USGC-mirror opportunity
  (below).
  [business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/news/india-exports-record-4-8-mn-tonnes-oilmeal-animal-feed-in-fy24-sea-124041800552_1.html)

## The demand-tier map

Three tiers by *how the demand is secured* — the surest first:

### Tier A — Captive government demand (surest; the state is maker and buyer)
| Product | Captive demand | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| **Fortified rice kernel (FRK)** | **~3.5–4 LMT/yr** standing (1% of ~350–400 LMT NFSA rice); program **₹17,082 cr to Dec-2028, 100% GoI-funded** | **Verified, mandated, quantified** |
| Fortified atta | ~2.0–2.5 LMT/yr estimated; **voluntary today** — a mandate converts it to captive | Reported (would need mandate) |
| Fortification inputs (natural vit-E from bran DD) | Captive if the state makes its own fortificant instead of importing 60–70% | Estimated |

**This tier needs no market** — the demand is the welfare programme. FRK is the single surest line
in the entire high-margin set: government-created, government-bought, funded to 2028.

### Tier B — Domestic-commercial growth (real demand, market-dependent, capex/quality-gated)
| Product | India market → | CAGR |
|---|---|---|
| **Rice bran oil** | ~USD 1.39 bn (2024) | ~3% |
| **Poha / murmura FMCG** | packaged rice 13.68→21.09 MT; ethnic snacks USD 5.18→17.5 bn | 4.75% / 14.5% |
| **Maize starch + derivatives** | USD 3.45→4.12 bn (starch+deriv) | 5.2–7.3% |
| **Vital wheat gluten** (import-subst) | niche now; ~13,000 t/yr imports to displace | 4.6% (global) |
| Besan / gram flour | >5 mn t domestic | ~5.6% (global) |

### Tier C — Export-led (the growth is offshore; brand/certification/market-development needed)
| Product | Export status | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Basmati rice** | **₹50,000 cr / USD 5.94 bn (FY25)** | Proven, largest — the benchmark |
| **Plant / pea protein isolate** | shipping to US/UK/EU/UAE; ~9.4% export CAGR | Fastest global demand (10.8–14.1%) |
| Spices (value-added) | **₹39,994 cr (FY25 record)** | Value-addition proven |
| Nutraceuticals (oryzanol/vit-E) | India *imports* today — export potential from bran | Highest ₹/kg, tiny tonnage |
| Green silica | export/import-subst; tyre-led | 10.4% CAGR, corporate capex flowing |
| Non-basmati rice / oilmeal / besan | ₹55,408 cr / ₹15,368 cr / diaspora | Volume floor, already monetised |

## The export headline

**Strongest export upside, ranked:**

1. **Basmati rice — the proven case.** ₹50,000 cr FY25, 15.7% volume growth, 154 countries, ₹82/kg
   realisation. Nothing else in the book exports at this scale or premium. The template for
   "process-and-brand pays."
2. **Plant / pea protein isolate — the highest-growth demand.** A USD 27→79 bn global plant-protein
   market growing 14% a year, pea isolate at 10.8%, India already exporting off a pulse base it
   procures. This is the clearest *new* export India is under-exploiting.
3. **Green silica from husk — the industrial export with capex already moving.** 10.4% CAGR,
   tyre-led, and the world-first commercial plant is being built *in India* (Tata Chem ₹775 cr;
   Goodyear + IISc). Import-substitution and export both.
4. **Nutraceuticals (natural vitamin E / oryzanol) — the high-value irony.** India makes the world's
   RBO yet *imports* the oryzanol and 60–70% of its natural vit-E. Turning bran deodoriser-distillate
   into an export fortificant is high-₹/kg, low-tonnage — a strategic export, not a volume one.

**Tie to APEDA's goal and the USGC gap.** APEDA targets **USD 100 bn agri-exports by 2030** (USD 55 bn
from APEDA-scheduled products), diversifying into EU/USA/UK/Oceania/Japan/Korea
([ruralvoice / freshplaza](https://www.freshplaza.com/asia/article/9749030/india-targets-100b-agri-exports-by-2030/)).
But [agri-trader-playbook.md §5](agri-trader-playbook.md) documents the structural gap: **APEDA is a
generalist promotion body with no permanent overseas market-development offices and no technical
demand-creation programs** — the mirror-opposite of the US Grains Council, which runs a funded New
Delhi office and feeding-trial programs *to open India's market*. The processed/value-added lines
above (protein isolate, green silica, RBO, value-added spices, DORB into SEA aqua-feed) are exactly
the products that would benefit from a USGC-style, commodity-deep, in-market demand-creation push —
and exactly what India does not yet fund. **The export upside is real; the export *promotion* for
processed value-added is the white space.**

## Which products the operational Mega Food Parks should host

**Infrastructure state (verified):** **41 Mega Food Parks approved, 25 operational as of 31 Aug 2025
across 17 states** ([mofpi.gov.in](https://www.mofpi.gov.in/en/Schemes/mega-food-parks)). Scheme
grant **50% (75% in difficult areas), max ₹50 cr** per park; **discontinued for new parks from
01.04.2021** (committed liabilities only), succeeded by PMKSY Agro-Processing Clusters + PMFME +
PLISFPI. The standard central-processing facility already specifies **multigrain milling + oil
extraction + animal-feed units (~26 TPH / 200 TPD)** — i.e. the rice/oilseed/grain backbone is
built into the park template, not an add-on.

**Operational parks with a grain / rice / oilseed anchor (from MoFPI/PMFIAS/Wikipedia lists —
*reported*, some park detail dated):**

| Park | State | Grain/oil relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Srini Mega Food Park (Chittoor) | Andhra Pradesh | grain/general CPC; AP rice belt |
| Godavari Mega Aqua Park (Bhimavaram) | Andhra Pradesh | aqua + DORB/rice-bran feed linkage |
| Jangipur Bengal MFP (Murshidabad) | West Bengal | **explicit rice-processing unit** |
| Indus Best / Avantee MFP (Khargone / Dewas) | Madhya Pradesh | wheat/soy/grain belt, oil extraction |
| International MFP (Fazilka) | Punjab | rice/wheat bowl, milling |
| India Food Park (Tumkur) | Karnataka | multigrain + oil CPC |
| North East MFP (Nalbari) | Assam | rice, oilseed |
| HTKR MFP | Maharashtra | agro-food, grain/oil hub |

**Best-fit hosting recommendation (margin × demand × export, from this report + the ranking):**

- **Poha / murmura FMCG unit** — *host first.* Tiny ₹21-lakh capex, **10–15% net (highest DPR
  margin)**, domestic FMCG + diaspora export demand, and the park's packing/cold-chain/market
  linkage is exactly what an FMCG line needs. The cleanest margin-plus-demand-plus-export triple.
- **Rice-bran-oil solvent-extraction + refining unit** — *host as the anchor.* Largest
  import-substitution (~₹7,544 cr), deepest price-inelastic offtake, real export, and it bolts onto
  the park's oil-extraction CPC. Pair it with a **DORB → feed/export** stream.
- **FRK unit** — *host for guaranteed volume.* Captive ₹2,700 cr/yr demand funded to 2028; a park
  co-located with milling can supply blenders regionally. Surest offtake even if margin is policy-set.
- **Maize wet-milling / starch** — *host only where the maize belt + ₹80–120 cr capital exist*
  (MP/Punjab parks); real demand, but capital-heavy, so a park anchor tenant not an SME unit.
- **Green silica from husk** — *co-locate with a rice park's boiler/husk stream* where a corporate
  anchor (Tata-Chem-style) can take the capex; the demand is offshore/industrial, the feedstock is
  the park's husk.
- **Plant/pea protein isolate** — *host in a pulse-belt park (MP/Rajasthan/Karnataka)* as the
  export-growth bet; needs certification and export-market development (the APEDA/USGC gap).

**Do NOT build the park case on the nutraceutical tail** (oryzanol, tocotrienols, standalone vit-E):
high ₹/kg, immaterial tonnage, no bankable Indian DPR — a garnish extractable *inside* the RBO
refinery, not a standalone park unit. This matches the ranking's and byproduct-tree's conclusion.

## Caveats

Market-size and CAGR figures for RBO, poha/snacks, starch/derivatives, gluten, protein isolate,
nutraceuticals, green silica and besan are **reported** (private research houses — IMARC, Mordor,
IndustryARC, Fortune, Straits, Persistence, Intel, Verified) and should be read as
order-of-magnitude; competing houses disagree by 2–5× on some lines (RBO CAGR 1.7–11.1%; modified
starch USD 536 mn vs 13.4 bn depending on scope definition). Export figures for **basmati,
non-basmati, oilmeal, spices, wheat-gluten imports and the FRK program are verified** (APEDA,
DGCI&S, SEA, Spices Board, PIB) but vintages differ (basmati/non-basmati/spices FY25; oilmeal FY24;
besan trade data dated 2015-16 / Oct23-Sep24). Poha- and rice-starch-specific export volumes are
**not broken out** in trade data — they sit inside "snacks" / "misc processed" aggregates, so their
export lines are inferred, not isolated. Mega Food Park operational counts are verified (MoFPI, 25 of
41, Aug-2025); individual park *anchor-commodity* detail is **reported** and partly dated (PMFIAS 2016,
Wikipedia lists). Cross-referenced margins, savings and the product map live in
[product-margins-ranked.md](product-margins-ranked.md),
[consolidated-savings-by-crop.md](consolidated-savings-by-crop.md) and
[byproduct-product-tree.md](byproduct-product-tree.md) and are not re-derived here.
