# PPP & government-cooperative-private rice milling / grain processing in India

*Complement to [rice-mill-business-model.md](rice-mill-business-model.md) (a cooperative
1-TPH DPR). That file covers the small cooperative mill; **this one covers larger-scale
PPP and government-cooperative-private models** — who builds them, on what concession
model, and whether they mill for FCI/PDS. Each example is labelled **VERIFIED** (primary
or reputable secondary source with date), **REPORTED** (credible news, single source), or
**UNVERIFIED** (claim seen but not corroborated). Recommendation section is kept separate
from fact.*

Research date: 2026-07-24. All URLs and vintages inline.

---

## Headline finding (the framing this whole file argues)

**India does NOT have a basic rice-milling capacity gap.** It has ~100,000+ private rice
mills, and government paddy is milled almost entirely through **Custom Milled Rice (CMR)** —
i.e. FCI and state agencies *outsource* milling to private millers at a fixed conversion
charge; **FCI owns essentially no mills of its own.** So "PPP for rice milling" in the plain
sense (build a mill to grind government paddy) is **largely redundant** — CMR already is a
government-private arrangement, just not badged "PPP."

Where PPP/large-capital structures actually show up is **adjacent to milling, not in milling
itself**:
- **Storage** — silos and godowns (Odisha rice-storage PPP; FCI hub-and-spoke steel silos;
  PEG scheme; PACS "world's largest grain storage plan"). This is the real PPP frontier.
- **Value-addition / modern integrated mills** — parboiling, fortification (FRK), rice-bran
  oil, husk cogeneration. This is where private capital under-invests and where a PPP or
  subsidy vehicle *could* fit — but current Indian instruments here are **subsidy/incentive**
  (capital grant, VGF-style) rather than concession-PPP.

So the honest answer to "is PPP milling needed?": **not for basic CMR (saturated), yes-ish
for modern integrated biorefinery-mills and storage — and even there India is using
subsidy/guarantee tools more than classic BOT/DBFOT concessions.** Detail below.

## 1. State-government modern/mega rice-mill & storage PPP

### 1.1 Odisha Rice Storage PPP — the cleanest real "rice PPP" (VERIFIED)
- **What:** Odisha State Civil Supplies Corp (OSCSC), advised by **IFC**, bid out
  **scientific rice-storage depots** to private entrepreneurs.
- **Model:** design-build-finance-operate-maintain (DBFOM-style; document does not use a
  crisp BOT label). Private operator finances construction, procures land, operates &
  maintains, keeps rice to national quality standard; OSCSC pays **storage rental per MT/yr**
  (lowest-quote wins) plus a **capital subsidy of US$35/MT** in two instalments.
- **Capacity / investment:** **28 locations, ~2,500 MT each = ~70,000 MT** added storage;
  **~US$3 million** private investment from *small local* entrepreneurs (not big corporates).
- **Bidders:** 41 qualified; **28 sites won by 22 different bidders** (deliberately
  anti-concentration — opposite of the Adani silo story below).
- **Timeline:** Phase-1 concessions **signed 2015**; Phase-2 **March 2017**.
- **Milling linkage:** **STORAGE ONLY — no milling.** It stores CMR/rice in the PDS chain.
- Source: World Bank/IFC brief, *India: Odisha Rice Storage PPP*, 2018
  (documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/191091542223698017 — .txt version;
  ifc.org/.../2018-india-odisha-rice-storage-ppp-brief.pdf). **VERIFIED.**
- More recently OSCSC is again procuring a DEA-empanelled transaction advisor for **godowns
  under the Private Entrepreneur Guarantee (PEG) scheme in PPP mode** (oscsc.in, tender docs
  2024–25). **REPORTED.**

### 1.2 Odisha — "400+ new rice mills, 50% subsidy" to absorb surplus paddy (REPORTED)
- Odisha's paddy surplus has outrun milling capacity; state signalled it needs **400+ new
  rice mills** and would offer up to **50% capital subsidy** to attract private millers.
- This is a **subsidy-for-private-capacity** model, **not** a concession PPP, and **not**
  state-owned mills — OSCSC still runs on custom-milling by private mills.
- Source: news video title "Odisha Needs 400+ New Rice Mills… Govt to Offer 50% Subsidy"
  (YouTube, surfaced in search; not independently corroborated). **REPORTED / weak — treat
  the 400 figure as indicative.**

### 1.3 Telangana — paddy-glut-driven modern-mill & value-addition push (REPORTED)
- Telangana produces **~300 lakh t paddy/yr**, ~60% of India's *Rabi* paddy procurement;
  parboiled-mill build-out was explosive — **~2,500 mills (1,500 parboiled + 1,000 raw)**,
  "more than half… set up in the last three years," each parboiled unit **~₹10 crore**.
- 2025–26 policy signals (Minister Uttam Kumar Reddy): incentive policy for **export-oriented
  modern mills** (controlled whitening, precision polishing, nutrient-retention, AI QA) and a
  call for **private investors to build large-capacity storage under PPP models**.
- **Model:** again **incentive/subsidy for mills + PPP for storage**, not concession-milling.
- Sources: Deccan Chronicle "Uttam Calls For Modernising Rice Storage, Value Addition"
  (deccanchronicle.com, 2025); Kishore Infratech cost note 2026; PressReader (parboiled-mill
  count, 2022). **REPORTED.**

### 1.4 Chhattisgarh — Markfed / NAN cooperative custom milling (VERIFIED as CMR, not PPP)
- Chhattisgarh's paddy (targeting **16 MnT** procurement KMS 2025-26) is milled by **private
  registered custom millers**; **Markfed** allocates milling between **NAN (Nagrik Aapurti
  Nigam / Civil Supplies Corp)** for state pool and **FCI** for central pool. Outturn: 67 kg
  raw / 68 kg parboiled rice per quintal, deposited to NAN & FCI.
- Miller registers with District Collector; Collector issues milling permit. **This is
  classic CMR — a government-cooperative-private arrangement but NOT badged PPP and NOT
  state-owned mills.**
- Sources: cgmarkfed.in custom-milling page; BigMint "Chhattisgarh sets milling roadmap for
  Kharif 2025-26" (bigmint.co, 2025). **VERIFIED (as CMR).** No evidence found of a
  Chhattisgarh state-owned modern integrated mill in Nava Raipur (searched, nothing). The
  cooperative 1-TPH DPR in the sister file (NCDC/CSISAC) is the closest CG cooperative-mill
  instance.

### 1.5 Punjab — tech-driven custom-milling policy (VERIFIED, but it's CMR digitised)
- Punjab Cabinet approved **Custom Milling Policy KMS 2024-25** (CM Bhagwant Mann): paddy
  from **Pungrain/Markfed/Punsup** milled by private millers → central pool; whole chain
  e-monitored via **Anaj Kharid portal + Vaahan app + vehicle tracking**; online RO-scheme
  allotment of paddy to mills.
- **Model:** technology-enabled CMR. **Not PPP, not state mills** — private millers, state
  procurement, digital governance.
- Sources: Tribune "Punjab Cabinet nod to tech-driven rice milling policy"
  (tribuneindia.com, 2024); Daily Pioneer, Punjab News Express (2024). **VERIFIED (as CMR).**

### 1.6 West Bengal — BENFED tripartite cooperative milling (VERIFIED as CMR)
- **BENFED** (WB State Cooperative Marketing Federation) procures paddy at MSP via PACS;
  **tripartite agreement (BENFED ↔ cooperative society ↔ rice mill)** tags paddy to a mill;
  miller delivers **75% CMR to Central Pool**. E-Paddy digital platform.
- **Model:** cooperative-anchored CMR, **not PPP.**
- Sources: benfed.org marketing-procurement; food.wb.gov.in; WB Custom Milled Rice
  (Obligation & Control) Order 2015. **VERIFIED (as CMR).**

## 2. FCI / Central — does FCI own mills? And the storage-PPP frontier

### 2.1 FCI owns ~no mills — CMR is the model (VERIFIED)
- FCI procures ~20–25% of rice production at MSP and gets it milled by **private millers
  under custom milling**; it does **not** run its own rice mills at scale. Recurrent CMR
  friction (godown/GST/FRK, delivery windows) is well documented.
- Sources: DCMSME rice-milling profile (dcmsme.gov.in); Tribune CMR reports 2024-25.
  **VERIFIED.** → **Implication: there is no "FCI milling PPP" to speak of; the government's
  milling PPP is CMR by another name.**

### 2.2 FCI Hub-and-Spoke **steel-silo** PPP — the big-ticket "PPP" (VERIFIED; STORAGE not milling)
- **What:** modern **steel silos** for wheat/grain storage on **DBFOO / DBFOT** (Design-
  Build-Finance-Own-Operate, some Own-Operate-Transfer), private capital, **hub** silos
  (with container depot) + **spoke** silos (without).
- **Scale:** programme of **~134 silo contracts, ~60 LMT, ~₹20,000 crore**; govt-approved
  action plan for **100 LMT of silos in PPP** overall.
- **Concentration (the controversy):** **Adani Agri Logistics + Leap India together won ~110
  of 134 contracts, >₹16,500 cr, ~46.5 of 60 LMT** — FCI's own proposed **anti-monopoly
  clause was dropped** at a 2022 meeting; farmer body **AIKS alleges an Adani-Leap duopoly.**
- **Live units (VERIFIED, commissioned 2024):** 6 silos × 50,000 MT = 300,000 MT across
  **Bihar, Punjab, Gujarat** — Darbhanga & Samastipur (**Adani**, Apr–May 2024, Darbhanga has
  rail siding), Sahnewal (**Leap**, DBFOT) & Baroda (**Leap**), Amritsar & Batala (**NCML**),
  most completed May–Jun 2024.
- **Milling linkage:** **STORAGE ONLY.** Silos hold procured grain; no milling.
- Sources: PIB PRID 2059336 (2024, 403 on direct fetch but indexed); DD News "FCI enhances
  storage… through PPP in three states" (ddnews.gov.in, 2024, VERIFIED unit-level detail);
  adaniports.com LoA release; Newslaundry "anti-monopoly clause gone…" (2026) &
  thenewsminute.com (concentration); dailypioneer.com (AIKS). **VERIFIED (facts) /
  REPORTED (duopoly characterisation).**

### 2.3 Private Entrepreneurs Guarantee (PEG) scheme — the pre-silo storage PPP (VERIFIED)
- Since **2008**: private party builds godown under PPP; **FCI guarantees ≥10 yr hire**
  (conventional godown) or **30 yr** (modern steel silo) as return on investment. **20 lakh MT
  of silos** sanctioned within PEG. CWC (~130 lakh MT) also modernising godowns under PPP.
- **Storage, not milling.** Sources: PIB storage releases; factly.in; World Bank PPP
  knowledge lab (Punjab grain silos). **VERIFIED.**

## 3. Cooperative + private

### 3.1 "World's Largest Grain Storage Plan in Cooperative Sector" — PACS godowns (VERIFIED)
- Approved **31 May 2023**; **700 lakh MT** decentralised storage over 5 yrs, **~₹1.25 lakh
  crore**, at **PACS level** — godowns + **custom hiring centres + processing units** — via
  convergence of existing schemes, **NCDC-led, NABARD-supported.**
- **Pilot:** godowns done in **11 PACS across 11 states = 9,750 MT** created (PM inaugurated
  Feb 2024).
- **Milling linkage:** the plan explicitly includes **"processing units" at PACS**, so
  **PACS-as-rice-mill is on-menu** — but the pilot delivered *storage*, not mills, so
  PACS-run milling is **so far aspirational, not demonstrated at scale.**
- Sources: PIB PRID 2115198 / 2146718; Business Standard 24 Feb 2024; DD News; PWOnlyIAS.
  **VERIFIED (plan + pilot); PACS-milling = ANNOUNCED / not yet built.**

### 3.2 PACS diversification / model bye-laws (VERIFIED)
- Ministry of Cooperation circulated **model bye-laws (5 Jan 2023)** letting PACS run **25+
  activities incl. godowns, foodgrain procurement, custom hiring centres, processing.**
  15 Feb 2023 plan: new multipurpose PACS in every panchayat over 5 yrs.
- **This is the legal enabler for PACS to own mills/processing** — but again, enablement, not
  built capacity. Sources: PIB PRID 2099568; cooperation.gov.in. **VERIFIED (enabler).**

### 3.3 NAFED / NCCF "Bharat Rice" — cooperative-branded, privately milled (VERIFIED)
- FCI gives OMSS rice to **NAFED, NCCF, Kendriya Bhandar** (~5 LMT phase 1) to sell as
  **"Bharat Rice" @ ₹29/kg** in 5/10 kg packs. **NCCF/NAFED do NOT mill** — they float
  **tenders to empanelled private millers** (e.g. NCCF Noida: mill 25,500 MT into Bharat
  Rice; miller does milling+lifting+distribution).
- **Model:** cooperative brand + procurement, **private milling on tender** — a
  government-cooperative-private processing tie-up, not a PPP asset.
- Sources: nccf-india.com tender notice; indiancooperative.com; PIB PRID 2022646;
  nafed-india.com miller-application PDF (2025). **VERIFIED.**

### 3.4 FPO-run rice mills — 10,000 FPO scheme / SFAC (VERIFIED as capability, thin on mills)
- Central Sector Scheme "Formation & Promotion of **10,000 FPOs**" (2020, **₹6,865 cr** to
  2027-28); **10,000th FPO** (Khagaria, Bihar) is **maize/banana/paddy**-focused; FPOs get
  **≤₹18 lakh/FPO** + **credit guarantee ≤₹2 cr** and support for **processing/value-add.**
- **Milling linkage:** paddy FPOs *can* build small mills with this support, but the scheme
  is FPO-formation, not a milling-PPP; documented FPO rice mills exist but are small and
  scattered. Sources: PIB PRID 2106913 (Feb 2025); sfacindia.com strategy paper. **VERIFIED
  (scheme); FPO milling = enabled, small-scale.**

## 4. Mega Food Parks with rice processing (VERIFIED model; rice-anchor examples thin)

- **MoFPI Mega Food Park Scheme** *is* a PPP: implemented by an **SPV** (body corporate;
  state/cooperative SPVs exempt from separate SPV), **capital grant 50% (75% hilly/NE), max
  ₹50 cr/project**, EoI for **JV partners from private corporates on PPP mode**, with an
  **Anchor Investor** processing unit commissioned with the park.
- **Rice-anchor linkage:** the scheme supports rice/paddy processing as an anchor/units, but
  the scheme is generic food-processing; **specific rice-anchored mega food parks were not
  cleanly isolated** in this search (several parks include rice/dal units but none surfaced as
  a verified "rice-anchor mega food park"). Source: mofpi.gov.in revised MFPS guidelines
  (2016); MoFPI scheme pages. **VERIFIED (model); rice-anchor example UNVERIFIED here.**

## 5. International / comparative PPP milling (brief)

### 5.1 Nigeria — Integrated Rice Mills on BOO PPP (REPORTED/VERIFIED-secondary)
- Federal Govt approved **10 Integrated Rice Mills under PPP on Build-Operate-Own (BOO)**;
  one commissioned at **Sheda, Kwali (Abuja).** Backed by CBN **Anchor Borrowers' Programme**
  (2015) linking smallholders → milling.
- **Olam Nigeria:** greenfield **US$49.2 m** integrated **6,000-ha irrigated paddy farm +
  mill, ~36,000 t milled rice/yr**, nucleus-and-outgrower model (private, IFAD/Govt VCDP
  partnership). Sources: africa-newsroom.com / zawya.com (FG BOO mills); olamgroup.com press
  releases; allAfrica (ABP). **REPORTED (FG mills) / VERIFIED (Olam figures).**
- **Lesson for India:** Nigeria used **BOO PPP + anchor-borrower credit** precisely because it
  *lacked* a private milling base. India has the opposite problem (milling saturated), so the
  Nigeria model argues **against** replicating basic-milling PPP in India and **for** the
  farm-linkage / outgrower + value-addition parts.

### 5.2 Integrated rice-mill biorefinery economics (VERIFIED academic)
- Peer-reviewed integrated rice-mill biorefinery (husk→boiler/steam-turbine cogen + bran→RBO
  + broken-rice valorisation): **EBITDA ~US$15.5 m/yr, IRR ~21.2%, payback ~3.4 yr.** Confirms
  the byproduct-capture thesis is bankable at integrated scale.
- Source: MDPI *Resources* 2025, "Integrated Sustainability Assessment of a Rice Mill
  Biorefinery" (doi 10.3390/resources15020028). **VERIFIED (academic model, not an Indian
  built plant).**

## 6. WHY PPP for rice milling — or why not (analysis, kept separate from fact)

**The gap is NOT basic milling.** ~100,000+ private mills + CMR already convert government
paddy. Adding PPP *basic* mills would subsidise capacity into a saturated, low-margin segment
that private capital already fills. **PPP for plain CMR milling is not needed.**

**The gap IS three adjacent things, and only two suit PPP:**

1. **Modern storage (silos/godowns)** — genuine PPP fit, already happening (FCI hub-spoke
   DBFOT/DBFOO, PEG 30-yr silo guarantee, Odisha rice-storage PPP, PACS godowns). The
   **cautionary tale is concentration** (Adani-Leap ~110/134 silos after the anti-monopoly
   clause was dropped) vs Odisha's deliberately fragmented 22-bidder model. **Design PPPs like
   Odisha, not like the silo duopoly.**

2. **Modern integrated / value-added milling (parboiling + fortification + RBO + husk power)**
   — this is where private millers under-invest (bran stabilisation, solvent-extraction RBO,
   cogen have scale/capex thresholds small millers won't cross). **This is the natural home
   for a PPP or VGF/subsidy vehicle** — but note India today reaches for **capital-subsidy /
   incentive** tools (Odisha 50%, Telangana export incentives, MoFPI 50-75% grant, 10,000-FPO
   grants) **rather than concession PPP.** A concession-PPP (state supplies paddy/husk feed +
   guaranteed FRK/RBO offtake, private builds integrated biorefinery-mill) is **plausible and
   largely untried** in India.

3. **Fortification (FRK) for PDS** — already a government-private tie-up (FRK 1:100 blend,
   private FRK manufacturers, mills blend, FCI/state tests). Could be structured as a
   fortification-PPP but currently runs on procurement + specification, not concession.

**Where PPP fits the project's byproduct-capture (RBO, husk power) thesis:**
- The sister DPR shows a **1-TPH cooperative mill** works on its own economics (IRR 19%) and
  captures bran/husk at small scale. The **RBO/solvent-extraction and husk-cogen steps** are
  what need **integrated scale + capital** the cooperative can't self-fund.
- **Recommended vehicle:** a **cluster/anchor PPP or JV** — cooperative/PACS/FPO supplies
  aggregated paddy & bran; a private solvent-extraction + cogen partner builds the RBO plant
  and captive power; state provides **VGF/capital grant + a PDS fortified-rice or RBO offtake
  guarantee** (the guarantee is the PPP's revenue backbone, exactly as PEG's hire-guarantee
  underwrites silos). This is **PPP-appropriate**; basic CMR milling is not.

**One-line rule of thumb:** *Use PPP where the asset is capital-heavy AND private capital
under-invests AND government can guarantee offtake/hire (silos, integrated RBO/husk
biorefinery, fortification-for-PDS). Do NOT use PPP for basic paddy-to-rice milling — CMR and
~100k private mills already cover it.*
