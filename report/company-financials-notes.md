# Agri-Processing Company Financials — "Buy Grain → Process → Sell" Returns

**Purpose:** Estimate the real returns of the grain/edible-oil value chain (buy — incl. FCI OMSS
— process, sell) using the published financials of India's major agri-processors and traders.
**Compiled:** 2026-07-24. Data in `data/agri_company_financials.csv`. Every figure cited with
source + FY; estimates/derived figures are labelled UNVERIFIED.

## KEY DERIVED INSIGHT — the margin ladder

The single clearest finding: **margin is a function of how much brand/processing value is added,
not of scale.** Three tiers emerge cleanly from the numbers.

**Tier 1 — Pure commodity trading / crushing (EBITDA 2–4%, PAT 0–2%, often loss-making):**
- Cargill India: FY24 operating margin **3%**; FY23 PAT **−₹113 cr** (loss); FY22 PAT ₹99 cr
  (0.7%). A ₹13,500–14,500 cr revenue business that oscillates around break-even.
- Bunge India: FY24 operating margin **2.3%**, PAT **0.6%**; FY23 was a **loss** (PAT −₹52 cr,
  −4.3%). Management itself guides to "operating profitability of 3–4%."
- AWL *at the group level* (63,672 cr, 3.9% EBITDA, 1.9% PAT) sits here too — because ~65–70% of
  its revenue is edible-oil, which is a refining/trading spread business.
- Read-across: **the raw OMSS-buy-vs-mandi-sell arbitrage, or oilseed crush, earns a 1–4% EBITDA
  spread that is fully exposed to price swings** — a good year (moderating oilseed prices, FY24)
  and a bad year (FY23) flip the same business from profit to loss. This is the "MSV" thin-margin
  reality: trading grain is a low-single-digit, high-volatility spread, not a durable return.

**Tier 2 — Branded staple processing (EBITDA 11–12%, PAT 7–9%, ROCE 12–19%):**
- KRBL (India Gate): FY25 EBITDA **12%**, PAT **8.5%**, ROCE 12% (down from FY24's 15%/11%/16%
  as basmati realisations softened).
- LT Foods (Daawat): FY25 EBITDA **11.3%**, PAT **7.1%**, ROCE **19%**, gross margin ~35%.
- This is where the money is. A branded rice miller earns **3–5x the EBITDA margin** of a raw
  grain trader on the *same underlying grain*. The delta between LT Foods' ~35% gross margin and
  its ~11% EBITDA margin is brand-building/distribution spend — i.e. the value-add is real and
  defended by marketing, not just milling.
- Caveat: high inventory intensity. KRBL carries **358 inventory-days**, LT Foods **286** —
  basmati must be aged, so working capital is heavy and ROCE (12–19%) is meaningfully below the
  EBITDA margin. The return on *capital* is good but not spectacular once you fund the ageing rice.

**Tier 3 — AWL's Foods & FMCG sub-segment (the branded part inside a commodity company):**
- AWL's blended 3.9% EBITDA hides a mix: the edible-oil "Kitchen Essentials" leg is Tier-1 spread
  business, but the **Foods & FMCG segment grew 26% YoY** and is being built toward branded-staple
  economics. FY25 group PAT jumped 8x (₹148 cr → ₹1,226 cr) mostly on oil-price normalisation +
  scale, not a structural margin re-rate. AWL is the cleanest *listed* proxy for the whole
  "buy-process-sell staples" chain precisely because it spans both tiers — but its 3.9% blended
  margin should not be mistaken for the branded-foods margin.

**ITC Agri as the trading+processing comparator:**
- FY25 segment revenue ₹19,753 cr (+25% YoY), segment PBIT **+18% YoY**, PBIT margin improved
  ~368 bps. ITC does not disclose the absolute segment PBIT in the press release; its agri PBIT
  margin has historically run ~8–9%, *elevated by high-margin leaf-tobacco and value-added spice/
  coffee exports* — so it is NOT a clean read on plain wheat/rice trading, which sits lower.
  Treat ITC agri's ~8–9% as a blended trading+value-added-export number, above pure grain arbitrage.

## What this says about the FCI-OMSS "buy grain → process → sell" business

1. **Pure trading (OMSS buy vs mandi/open-market sell) is a 1–4% EBITDA game and periodically
   loss-making** — verified across Cargill (FY23 loss), Bunge (FY23 loss, FY24 2.3%), and AWL's
   oil leg. Price risk, not processing skill, dominates the P&L. A silo/OMSS arbitrage model
   should be underwritten at low-single-digit spreads with real downside years.

2. **Branding + processing is the value-capture layer: ~11–12% EBITDA, 7–9% PAT** (KRBL, LT
   Foods) — 3–5x the raw spread. The moat is brand + distribution + (for basmati) aged inventory,
   which also means heavy working capital (286–358 inventory-days) that pulls ROCE down to 12–19%.

3. **Storage/logistics (Adani Agri Logistics)** is a small, separate, capacity-based annuity
   (₹100–150 cr revenue, BOO contracts with FCI) — returns come from availability/throughput
   charges, not grain price. Absolute profitability is behind a paywall; flagged UNVERIFIED.

**Bottom line for the report:** the arbitrage of buying grain cheap (OMSS/MSP-linked) and selling
raw is structurally thin (1–4% EBITDA, volatile, occasionally negative). The durable return in
the grain chain is earned by converting the grain into a *brand* — rice millers show 8–14% EBITDA
was achievable historically and 11–12% currently. Any "buy-process-sell" economics should assume
Tier-1 (~2–4%) margins unless a defensible brand is built, in which case Tier-2 (~11–12% EBITDA,
7–9% PAT) is the realistic ceiling — funded by heavy ageing inventory.

## Data caveats / unverified flags

- **ITC Agri absolute PBIT:** only YoY growth (+18%) and margin direction (+368bps) are published;
  the ~8–9% margin and ~₹1,600 cr absolute PBIT in the CSV are **estimates, labelled UNVERIFIED**.
- **Cargill FY24 revenue** = CRISIL provisional; FY24 PAT not available (audited numbers pending
  at rating date). FY22/FY23 PAT are audited and verified.
- **LDC India & Olam Agri India:** ROC filings exist (FY25) but EBITDA/PAT are paywalled on Tofler/
  thecompanycheck; only revenue (LDC ₹12,645 cr; Olam >₹1,000 cr) is captured.
- **Adani Agri Logistics:** revenue band ₹100–150 cr; PAT +34.67% / EBITDA −12.71% YoY per Tofler
  but absolute figures paywalled — revenue band only, UNVERIFIED absolute.
- KRBL/LT Foods/AWL figures are from screener.in consolidated (auditor-reported); high confidence.
- ROE not separately broken out on screener for the single FY in several cases (shown as 3-yr avg).
