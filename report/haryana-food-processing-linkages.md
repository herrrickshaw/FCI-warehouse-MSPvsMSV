## 1. Haryana's Food Processing Policy Framework

### 1.1 The core instrument: Haryana Agri-Business and Food Processing Policy 2018

Notified 4 June 2018 (No.1/35/2017-41B-I, Industries & Commerce Dept.); in force "till amended or superseded" — no sunset date. Full text: [investharyana.in policy PDF](https://investharyana.in/content/pdfs/Haryana%20Agri%20Business%20and%20Food%20Processing%20Policy%202018%20.pdf) (extracted and read in full). Five-year goals (2018 vintage):

- Attract **₹3,500 cr investment** across the food-processing value chain; **20,000 jobs**; raise processing of perishables by **10%**; bring **14% of cropped area under horticulture** (from 7.58%); all 108 mandis on e-NAM ([policy PDF](https://investharyana.in/content/pdfs/Haryana%20Agri%20Business%20and%20Food%20Processing%20Policy%202018%20.pdf); also [KNN India, Jun 2018](https://knnindia.co.in/news/newsdetails/state/haryana-govt-notifies-agri-business-and-food-processing-policy-2018), [PR Haryana](https://prharyana.gov.in/en/haryana-government-has-notified-the-haryana-agribusiness-and-food-processing-policy-2018-which-is)).

**Fiscal incentives (verbatim from policy text):**

| Incentive | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capital investment subsidy | 25% of fixed capital investment, cap ₹1 cr, new units in "C"/"D" blocks; same 25%/₹1 cr for registered FPOs & agro/food cooperatives |
| Units inside food parks | 25% CIS (cap ₹1 cr in C/D blocks, ₹50 lakh in A/B) **+ 100% net-SGST reimbursement for 10 years**, capped at 100% of FCI |
| Cold chain / value-addition | 35% CIS, cap ₹5 cr (B/C/D blocks); reefer vehicles 50%, cap ₹50 lakh, max 4 per project |
| **Backward+forward linkage support** (§5.2.4) | 50% CIS on PPCs/collection centres at farm gate + distribution hubs/retail at front end; cap ₹2.5 cr (rural) / ₹3.5 cr (C/D blocks); max 20 projects in policy period |
| Mini Food Parks (§5.2.1) | C/D blocks only; min 10 acres, ≥5 units; 50% of project cost, cap **₹10 cr**, max 10 developers |
| Interest subsidy | 8% on term loan for women entrepreneurs/SHGs, up to ₹12 lakh/yr for 5 years |
| Market (mandi) fee | **100% exemption** on agri/horti produce used as raw material by in-state processors; Finance Dept reimburses HSAMB's shortfall on actuals (§5.3.7) |
| Other | 50% packaging-tech assistance (cap ₹20 lakh); milk-cess removal for secondary/tertiary dairy processing; CLU-charge exemption on agricultural land; seasonal-industry status (waiver of fixed power demand charges off-season, via HEPB); FAR relaxation to 150% |

**Critical carve-out (Annexure-1)**: mandi-fee exemption and policy benefits **exclude** oil extraction, rice shelling/grading/polishing, primary millet processing, all pulses/dal units, guar units, and flour/suji/maida manufacture — i.e., precisely the commodity-heavy first-stage processing that dominates Haryana's actual industry. This makes the headline "mandi fee exemption" far narrower than press coverage suggests.

**Newer policy status (flag)**: no notified successor to the 2018 policy found as of mid-2026; food processing also appears as a thrust sector in the Haryana Enterprise & Employment Policy 2020 framework, but claims of a distinct "AatmaNirbhar Haryana food processing policy" could not be verified from official sources. The 2018 policy remains the operative sector document per [haryanaindustries.gov.in](https://haryanaindustries.gov.in/) and [MSME Haryana](https://msme.haryana.gov.in/document/haryana-agri-business-and-food-processing-policy-2018/). Whether the ₹3,500 cr/20,000-job targets were achieved is **not publicly reported anywhere found — unverified**; no CAG performance audit specific to this policy surfaced.

### 1.2 Food parks and clusters

- **Operational HSIIDC parks**: Food Park **Rai** (Sonipat, ~120 acres) and **Saha** (Ambala) — the state's two "fully functional" parks, per [HSIIDC](https://hsiidc.org.in/activities-and-services/infrastructure-development-industrial-cluster-or-theme-parks/food-park-rai) and [PR Haryana](https://prharyana.gov.in/en/after-successfully-setting-up-of-two-fully-developed-food-parks-in-haryana-one-at-rai-distt-sonipat). Occupancy/capacity-utilization data is **not published — unverified**.
- **Mega Food Park, Barhi** (Sonipat, ~75 acres on NH-44): HSIIDC-implemented under MoFPI's Mega Food Park Scheme ([HSIIDC Barhi page](https://hsiidc.org.in/activities-and-services/infrastructure-development-industrial-cluster-or-theme-parks/mega-food-park-barhi)). MoFPI's list of 41 MFP projects (as of 31.01.2023) shows the two Haryana MFPs (Sonipat/HSIIDC, Rohtak/HAFED) as state-agency-implemented ([MoFPI list, Jan 2023](https://www.mofpi.gov.in/sites/default/files/list_of_mfp_on_31.01.2023.pdf)).
- **HAFED Mega Food Park, IMT Rohtak**: still **selling plots by e-auction as of January 2024** ([HAFED notice](http://hafed.gov.in/the-sale-of-industrial-plots-at-mega-food-park-rohtak-has-started-and-the-last-date-of-registration-for-e-auction-is-18012024)) — i.e., roughly a decade after sanction it had not filled. Listed on [India Investment Grid](https://indiainvestmentgrid.gov.in/opportunities/project/21779). The MFP scheme itself was **discontinued from 01.04.2021** (committed liabilities only), with 22–25 parks operational nationally ([PIB, Jul 2021](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=1737176), [MoFPI](https://www.mofpi.gov.in/en/Schemes/mega-food-parks)).
- **PMFME** (₹10,000 cr, FY21–FY25, ODOP approach): 59,202 micro units formalized nationally as of 31.12.2025 ([PIB](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2239684&reg=3&lang=2)); Haryana-specific sanction counts were not retrievable from official sources in this pass — **flagged**.
- **State scale**: Haryana hosts 8,000+ food-processing MSMEs and 40+ large units; contributes ~15.6% of central-pool foodgrains ([MoFPI Haryana state profile](https://www.mofpi.gov.in/sites/default/files/KnowledgeCentre/State%20Profile/Haryana.pdf), vintage ~2017; [foodprocessingindia.gov.in/state/haryana](https://www.foodprocessingindia.gov.in/state/haryana)).
- **Budget 2025-26 additions** (Mar 2025): guava processing/packaging plant at Hisar; kinnow juice plant at Sirsa (HAIC + dairy federation); largest cooperative mustard oil mill in south Haryana and a **sunflower oil mill in Kurukshetra under PPP**; ₹200 cr multi-feed (90 KLPD) ethanol plant at Panipat cooperative sugar mill ([Haryana Budget 2025-26 speech](https://cdnbbsr.s3waas.gov.in/s386e78499eeb33fb9cac16b7555b50767/uploads/2025/03/202503221525770346.pdf); [ChiniMandi](https://www.chinimandi.com/haryana-approves-rs-200-crore-multi-feed-ethanol-plant-at-panipat-sugar-mill/); [PRS analysis](https://prsindia.org/budgets/states/haryana-budget-analysis-2025-26)).

## 2. Backward Linkages

### 2.1 Contract farming
Haryana never enacted a standalone contract-farming act; it operates under the Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act 1961 as amended (Haryana Amendment Rules 2007 added contract-farming registration; sponsor registration cancellable on violation) ([HSAMB contract farming page](http://www.hsamb.org.in/contract-farming)). After the December 2021 repeal of the three central farm laws, the framework simply reverted to this pre-2020 APMC regime ([Shankar IAS](https://www.shankariasparliament.com/current-affairs/repeal-of-farm-laws)). The 2018 policy also flagged the **Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act (Haryana Amendment Bill 2017)** to enable long land leases to corporates without tenancy-rights transfer (policy §5.3.6) — enactment status **unverified**.

### 2.2 Crop diversification — Mera Pani Meri Virasat (MPMV)
₹7,000/acre DBT for replacing paddy with maize/cotton/bajra/pulses/vegetables; launched 2020 targeting dark-zone blocks (water table >40 m) ([scheme portal via sarkariyojana](https://sarkariyojana.com/haryana-mera-pani-meri-virasat-scheme/); [Krishi Jagran](https://krishijagran.com/agriculture-world/mera-pani-meri-virasat-scheme-haryana-government-is-providing-rs-7000-to-farmers-apply-soon/)). **Performance is weak**: a Tribune investigation found physical verification passed only **~19,670 acres** in a recent cycle, with big paddy districts (Sirsa 1,360 ac; Jind 694 ac) barely moving, versus Yamunanagar (5,245 ac) and Ambala (3,847 ac); officials attribute failure to uncertain returns on alternatives ([The Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/why-mera-pani-meri-virasat-scheme-has-failed-to-gain-farmers-support/); first-year enthusiasm ~26,000 farmers, [HT, May 2020](https://www.pressreader.com/india/hindustan-times-bathinda/20200531/282295322416820)). Implication for processors: the scheme has not yet created a reliable maize/millet raw-material base.

### 2.3 Bhavantar Bharpai Yojana (BBY)
Horticulture price-deficiency scheme paying the gap between mandi price and a notified floor by DBT within ~15 days; covers **21 horticulture crops** (potato, onion, tomato, cauliflower, kinnow, guava, turmeric etc.), designed to assure ~₹48,000–56,000/acre gross ([ekharid BBY page](https://ekharid.in/Home/BhavantarBharpaiiYojana); [Kshema overview](https://kshema.co/blogs/bhavantar-bharpai-yojana-haryana-farmers/)). **Bajra** was folded in: rather than full MSP purchase, HAFED bought at ~₹2,200/qtl with ₹300/qtl Bhavantar top-up to reach MSP ₹2,500 (KMS 2022 vintage; [Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/govt-stops-procurement-of-bajra-farmers-up-in-arms-440947)); ₹380 cr released to bajra growers in one tranche under BBY ([Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/bhavantar-payout-rs-380-crore-credited-to-bajra-growers/)). Field reporting found the deficiency-payment mechanics "still a work in progress" (registration/verification friction) ([ThePrint opinion, mandi visits](https://theprint.in/opinion/haryanas-deficit-payment-scheme-still-a-work-in-progress-i-learnt-in-my-mandi-visits/761153/)).

### 2.4 The "MSP for all crops" claim — verified with nuance
The claim "Haryana procures 14+ crops at MSP" is **correct and now superseded**: until August 2024 state agencies procured **14 crops** at MSP; CM Saini announced MSP for all crops on 4 Aug 2024 ([India TV](https://www.indiatvnews.com/haryana/haryana-becomes-first-state-in-india-to-buy-all-crops-from-farmers-at-msp-cm-nayab-singh-saini-bjp-assembly-elections-latest-updates-2024-08-04-945242)), and the **formal notification for 24 crops** came in December 2024 (adding ragi, soybean, nigerseed, safflower, barley, maize, jowar, jute, copra, summer moong) ([Business Standard, 22 Dec 2024](https://www.business-standard.com/india-news/haryana-govt-notifies-procurement-of-24-crops-at-minimum-support-price-124122200548_1.html); [Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/haryana-becomes-first-state-to-procure-all-24-crops-at-msp)). **Caveat**: notification ≠ purchase. The sunflower episode shows the gap — in 2023 HAFED offered ₹4,800/qtl against MSP ₹6,400 plus ₹1,000 Bhavantar, triggering farmer agitation at Kurukshetra ([Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/farmers-firm-on-demand-for-msp-sunflower-seeds-pile-up-at-mandis-515765)); HAFED later reaffirmed procurement at notified MSP (₹7,721/qtl for the current year, 2026 vintage) ([The Statesman](https://www.thestatesman.com/business/hafed-refutes-misleading-report-on-sunflower-msp-reaffirms-procurement-at-notified-rates-1503576685.html)).

## 3. Forward Linkages: The Government as Seller to Processors

The heart of the question: does the state already act as a bulk supplier to processing industry, and does it work?

### 3.1 FCI OMSS(D) — the flagship government-to-processor channel

- **Mechanics**: FCI sells central-pool wheat/rice by **weekly e-auction** (M-junction platform) exclusively to **flour mills, atta chakkis, processors and end-users** (traders were excluded from mid-2023); bids of 10–100 MT (raised to 200 MT at times to help small millers), buyer caps per PAN + FSSAI licence, monitoring against resale/diversion ([PIB, Dec 2024](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2078627); [Millennium Post](https://www.millenniumpost.in/business/govt-hikes-bid-quantity-to-200-tonne-under-omss-wheat-to-stabilise-wheat-flour-prices-538207)).
- **Volumes/prices**: 2023-24 cycle: offer raised to **101.5 LMT wheat to March 2024** (from 57 LMT), reserve price ₹2,150/qtl FAQ / ₹2,125 URS; ~71 LMT sold by early 2024, and AMFI credited it with cutting atta prices ₹6–8/kg ([PIB, Feb 2023](https://www.pib.gov.in/Pressreleaseshare.aspx?PRID=1901065); [Deccan Herald](https://www.deccanherald.com/business/markets/govt-sells-71-lakh-tonnes-wheat-so-far-in-open-market-2873501); [Tribune/AMFI](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/business/atta-prices-down-rs-6-8-per-kg-in-last-2-months-on-centres-move-to-sell-50-lakh-ton-wheat-in-open-market-flour-millers-body-488624)). **OMSS(D) 2024**: 25 LMT wheat at reserve ₹2,325 FAQ/₹2,300 URS to 31 Mar 2025 ([PIB](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2078627)). **2025-26**: wheat reserve price ₹2,550/qtl, expected 40–50 LMT from ~August ([Igrain](https://www.igrain.in/posts/wheat-open-market-sale-scheme-omss-2025-26-and)).
- **Rice tiering (Jan 2025 vintage)**: reserve price cut from ₹2,800 to **₹2,250/qtl for states (12 LMT cap) and ethanol distilleries (24 LMT cap)**; **₹2,400 for NAFED/NCCF/Kendriya Bhandar for "Bharat" brand**; **private traders/processors still ₹2,800** — valid to 30 Jun 2025 ([Business Standard, 17 Jan 2025](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/agriculture/centre-slashes-fci-rice-price-by-rs-550-per-quintal-to-rs-2-250-for-states-125011701436_1.html)).
- **Bharat Atta/Bharat Rice**: a semi-closed processing loop — FCI wheat at concessional rates to NAFED/NCCF/Kendriya Bhandar, milled and retailed at ₹27.50/kg atta (initial 2.5 LMT allocation, 100 mobile vans) ([IBEF](https://www.ibef.org/news/centre-has-launched-the-sale-of-bharat-atta-at-an-mrp-of-rs-27-50-kg)); notably, **private mills buying OMSS grain are barred from selling under the "Bharat" label** ([Rural Voice](https://eng.ruralvoice.in/national/govt-to-sell-wheat-and-rice-in-the-open-market-ban-on-bharat-flour-and-rice-for-private-mills.html)).
- **Known pathologies**: (i) when the OMSS reserve price sits **below the incoming-season MSP** (₹2,150 sale vs ₹2,275 wheat MSP for RMS 2024), farm unions allege it depresses mandi prices and creates a buy-cheap-from-FCI arbitrage against fresh procurement ([The Federal](https://thefederal.com/analysis/farmers-criticise-food-corporation-of-indias-bid-to-end-msp)); (ii) diversion/pilferage risk requiring FCI's PAN/FSSAI/inspection controls (same PIB/Millennium Post sources); (iii) June 2023: Centre **stopped OMSS sales to state governments** entirely to protect the price-control objective ([Adda247 summary](https://currentaffairs.adda247.com/centre-discontinues-sale-of-rice-and-wheat-under-omss-to-state-govts-to-check-price-rise/)) — showing the channel is policy-contingent, not contractually reliable for processors.

### 3.2 The rice-for-ethanol channel (precedent for state-steered offtake)

**Chronology (verified):**
- FCI rice sales to grain distilleries at a **fixed subsidized ₹2,000/qtl (₹20/kg)** underpinned early grain-ethanol capacity; allocations were **halted in July 2023** on food-security grounds amid low rice stocks and export bans ([Deccan Herald, Aug 2024](https://www.deccanherald.com/business/govt-allows-sale-of-23-lakh-tonnes-of-rice-from-food-corporation-of-indias-stock-to-ethanol-makers-3169620); [iamrenew on Food Secretary's refusal to resume](https://www.iamrenew.com/green-energy/food-secretary-thumbs-down-resuming-sale-of-subsidised-rice-for-ethanol/)).
- **Resumed August 2024**: with central-pool rice stocks swelling past ~540 LMT, Government allowed **23 LMT of FCI rice to ethanol makers via OMSS e-auctions (Aug–Oct 2024)** ([Deccan Herald](https://www.deccanherald.com/business/govt-allows-sale-of-23-lakh-tonnes-of-rice-from-food-corporation-of-indias-stock-to-ethanol-makers-3169620); [Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/india/excess-stock-govt-allows-distillers-to-lift-rice-for-producing-ethanol)).
- **ESY 2024-25 scale-up**: sanction of 24 LMT, then **+28 LMT taking the total to 52 LMT** for the year ([Sigma Earth](https://sigmaearth.com/union-government-approves-2-8-million-tonnes-of-fci-rice-for-ethanol-production-in-2024-25/), [Rice News Today](https://ricenewstoday.com/centre-allocates-2-8-million-tonnes-more-fci-rice-for-ethanol/)); a later report puts the cumulative FCI-rice-for-ethanol allocation at **72 LMT** ([ChiniMandi](https://www.chinimandi.com/centre-raises-fci-rice-allocation-for-ethanol-production-to-72-lakh-tonnes/) — treat the 52 vs 72 LMT figures as different cut-off dates; exact DFPD order-level reconciliation **not verified**).
- **Price flip-flops**: post-resumption rice went at the OMSS ₹2,800/qtl reserve — at which distilleries said rice-ethanol was uneconomic — then was **cut to a fixed ₹2,250/qtl nationwide (Jan 2025)** specifically to make distillery offtake viable ([ChiniMandi](https://www.chinimandi.com/government-reduces-price-of-fci-rice-for-ethanol-production-to-rs-2250-per-quintal/); [BioEnergy Times](https://bioenergytimes.com/relief-for-grain-based-ethanol-producers-as-govt-reduces-fci-rice-price-for-distilleries-producing-ethanol/)). OMCs even ran a dedicated tender cycle (Cycle 3, ~110 cr litres) **exclusively for FCI-rice ethanol**, and sought conversion of existing Q2–Q4 allocations to FCI-rice feedstock ([ChiniMandi tender coverage](https://www.chinimandi.com/esy-2024-25-omcs-invite-bid-for-around-124-crore-litres-of-ethanol-for-q2-and-q3/), [conversion EOI](https://www.chinimandi.com/omcs-seek-eoi-for-conversion-of-existing-allocations-of-q2-q3-and-q4-of-esy24-25-to-ethanol-from-fci-rice/)).

**Demand steering via differential ethanol pricing (ESY 2024-25 vintage):** OMC procurement prices are feedstock-linked — **maize ethanol ₹71.86/L, sugarcane-juice ~₹65.61/L, FCI-rice ethanol ₹60.32/L, C-heavy molasses ₹57.97/L** ([ThePrint](https://theprint.in/economy/rice-maize-or-sugarcane-indias-ethanol-push-triggers-debate-over-agricultural-concerns-subsidies/2967202/); [Business Standard on the ₹5.79/L maize hike](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/agriculture/omcs-hike-procurement-price-of-ethanol-produced-from-maize-by-rs-5-79-ltr-124010401139_1.html)). The price ladder plus feedstock-specific tenders lets the Centre dial distillery demand toward whichever grain the state wants to move — blending hit a record 18.2% in Dec 2024 ([ChiniMandi](https://www.chinimandi.com/esy-2024-25-omcs-invite-bid-for-around-124-crore-litres-of-ethanol-for-q2-and-q3/)).

**The maize pivot — both linkages built simultaneously:** In 2024, DFPD designated **NAFED and NCCF as maize aggregators for distilleries**: they procure maize at MSP (₹2,090/qtl in 2023-24 crop year) from pre-registered farmers and deliver to ethanol plants at a mutually agreed **₹2,291/qtl**; NCCF signed MoUs with distilleries on 21 Mar 2024 covering supply, logistics and pricing; both federations offer 100% assured MSP procurement for registered maize/tur/urad/masur growers (60 lakh farmers registered on their portals) ([iamrenew](https://www.iamrenew.com/green-energy/nafed-nccf-can-sell-maize-to-ethanol-makers-at-base-rate/); [SahkarNet](https://www.sahkarnet.com/national/govts-maize-push-for-ethanol/); [Indian Cooperative](https://www.indiancooperative.com/co-op-news-snippets/60-lakh-farmers-register-on-nccf-nafed-portals/)). This is the cleanest existing template of *government procuring crop X at an assured price and reselling to a processing industry whose demand is itself policy-created* (E20 blending mandate). Maize became the single largest ethanol feedstock in ESY 2024-25 ([Outlook Business](https://www.outlookbusiness.com/industry/maize-leads-esy-2024-25-ethanol-feedstock-shift)).

**Criticisms of the template (all sourced):** food-vs-fuel and groundwater objections to burning rice for ethanol ([Ideas for India](https://www.ideasforindia.in/topics/agriculture/why-indias-plan-to-sell-rice-for-ethanol-undermines-food-and-water-security); [Outlook Business](https://www.outlookbusiness.com/planet/industry/fci-rice-ethanol-impact-food-security)); ₹2,250/qtl is far below FCI's economic cost of rice (~₹4,000/qtl order of magnitude), so every quintal is a **hidden subsidy to distilleries** borne by the food subsidy bill (ThePrint, Ideas for India, above); diversion risk illustrated by the MP case where "5 lakh tonnes diverted" was alleged (FCI clarified actual quantum was 242.5 qtl — but the episode shows monitoring salience) ([ThePrint/FCI clarification](https://theprint.in/economy/fci-clarifies-rice-diversion-in-mp-ethanol-case-limited-to-242-50-quintals-not-5-lakh-tonnes-as-reported/2985619/)); and whiplash policy reversals (2023 pause → 2024 resume → price cuts) that stranded grain-distillery capex — the USDA GAIN Biofuels Annual documents the flip-flops ([USDA FAS IN2025-0031](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Biofuels+Annual_New+Delhi_India_IN2025-0031.pdf)).

### 3.3 HAFED as processor and seller

HAFED (turnover >₹10,000 cr, vintage per its site) is unusual among state federations in that it **forward-integrates itself** rather than selling raw stocks to private processors: Taraori basmati rice mill (consumer packs + exports; orders executed to Dubai and Saudi Arabia 2021–2024), 20-TPD flour mill at Taraori, oil mills at Rewari/Narnaul (mustard crushing **on commercial account**, i.e., HAFED buys seed for its own mills), a ₹7.6 cr turmeric plant at Radaur (3 MT/day + spices + 100 MT cold store), sugar mills, and branded consumer lines (basmati, kachchi ghani mustard oil, atta, sugar, turmeric) ([HAFED processing units](http://hafed.gov.in/processing-units-1), [rice mills](http://hafed.gov.in/rice-mills-0), [flour mill](http://hafed.gov.in/flour-mill), [turmeric plant](http://hafed.gov.in/turmeric-plant), [marketing/exports](http://hafed.gov.in/marketing-exports-0), [consumer products](http://hafed.gov.in/consumer-products)). For PSS crops it acts as NAFED's state agent (mustard, gram, sunflower) ([HAFED procurement page](http://hafed.gov.in/food-grains-procurement)). HAFED **has** used third-party e-mandi disposal — selling mustard and chana to millers/processors/corporates on AgriBazaar alongside NAFED ([AgriBazaar/Democratic Jagat](https://democraticjagat.com/news-post/3977/nafed-hafed-commence-the-sale-of-oil-seed-and-pulses-like-mustard-and-chana-on-agribazaar-platform)). But its non-MSP surplus disposal can be loss-making: procured bajra beyond PDS need was **resold into the market at a loss**, worsened by inflows from Rajasthan/Gujarat where no procurement operated ([ThePrint](https://theprint.in/opinion/haryanas-deficit-payment-scheme-still-a-work-in-progress-i-learnt-in-my-mandi-visits/761153/); [Tribune procurement-halt coverage](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/hafed-stops-bajra-purchase-441069)).

### 3.4 NAFED/NCCF pulses-to-millers
The Centre routinely directs NAFED/NCCF to dispose of buffer tur/chana **by online auction restricted to eligible dal millers** (milling into dal for consumers), plus negotiated supply to defence/paramilitary and states for PDS ([Milwaukee Sun/agency copy on tur release](https://www.milwaukeesun.com/news/273880515/centre-to-release-tur-dal-from-buffer-till-imported-stocks-arrive); [NewsOnAir Lok Sabha reply](https://www.newsonair.gov.in/the-stock-of-pulses-released-in-market-in-targeted-manner-to-control-prices-govt-in-ls); NAFED's NAFEX e-auction platform: [AgriBazaar blog](https://blog.agribazaar.com/now-nafed-to-launch-e-auction-platform-for-pulses-trade/)). Historical problem: buffer disposal timed badly can crash mandi prices exactly when farmers sell — a standard critique of NAFED disposals (specific Haryana-relevant volumes not verified in this pass).

### 3.5 Custom Milled Rice (CMR) — the inverse contract
In paddy, government is not seller but **bailor**: state agencies hand paddy to private mills, which must return **67 kg rice per 100 kg paddy** (with 1% FRK) to FCI on a staggered schedule (15% by Nov-end … 10% by 15 Mar). Haryana KMS 2024-25 experience: delivery start slipped a month (godown-assignment and FRK-supplier delays), policy was revised repeatedly, deadline extended to 30 June; ₹15/qtl bonus (₹3.14 cr total) paid to punctual millers; some millers were denied paddy allotments in Karnal/Kaithal ([Tribune explainer](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/why-fci-extended-cmr-delivery-deadline-and-what-is-means-for-haryanas-rice-millers/), [bonus report](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/millers-rewarded-for-timely-delivery-of-custom-milled-rice), [allotment disputes](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/haryana/16-millers-not-allotted-paddy-for-custom-milling-in-karnal-kaithal-549916/); [Haryana milling policy on hafed.gov.in](http://hafed.gov.in/sites/default/files/documents/revised-milling-policy.pdf)). CMR proves the state can contract the *entire* rice-milling industry annually — but also that margins, schedules and quality specs are chronic friction points.

### 3.6 Verdict on government-as-seller
It **works as a counter-cyclical release valve and a policy-steered offtake channel, not yet as a dependable commercial supply contract**. What functions: e-auction transparency; buyer-class restriction (processors only); tiered pricing by end-use; feedstock-linked demand creation (ethanol). What fails: (a) reserve prices are set for price control or stock clearance, not processor viability, so they whipsaw (₹2,800→₹2,250 rice; wheat OMSS suspended-to-states in 2023); (b) sales below economic cost embed opaque subsidies; (c) OMSS-below-MSP timing invites arbitrage against fresh procurement; (d) diversion/leakage requires heavy verification (PAN/FSSAI/inspections); (e) state-federation disposals (bajra) can be forced loss sales.

## 4. Evaluation: What Has Worked and What Hasn't in Haryana

- **ICRIER's MoFPI-commissioned evaluation of the Mega Food Park Scheme** (read in full: [MoFPI PDF](https://www.mofpi.gov.in/sites/default/files/icrierreportonimpactofmfpsfinal.pdf_0.pdf), vintage 2015-16, 16–17 SPVs surveyed) is damning on exactly the linkage question: **only one MFP nationally had an operational primary processing centre (PPC)**; PPC locations were consultant-chosen without feasibility studies; the scheme scored proposals on backward-linkage capex but had **no evaluation criterion at all for forward linkages**; SPVs ranked "formation of forward linkages" the **#1 critical success factor** and "no state-government support/coordination" the #1 negative; "getting units/agreements with food processors" was the second-worst-rated issue; no park became operational within the mandated 30 months. These structural findings frame why the Haryana MFPs (Barhi, Rohtak) also crawled — Rohtak still auctioning plots in Jan 2024 (HAFED notice above).
- **Investment vs target**: no official public accounting of the ₹3,500 cr / 20,000-job 2018 targets was found (**unverified/likely unreported**). The state's own showcase numbers remain the 3,000+ operative units (2018 policy text) and 8,000+ MSMEs (MoFPI profile, undated).
- **Diversification incentives underperform**: MPMV verified acreage (~19.7k acres) is a rounding error against ~13.5 lakh ha paddy; sunflower and bajra episodes show MSP-notification-vs-actual-purchase gaps.
- **What has worked**: mandi-fee exemption for fruit/vegetable processors (clean, self-executing); HAFED's own downstream units (basmati exports 2021–24, branded oils) as a public-sector proof that procurement-linked processing can run commercially; the wheat OMSS channel reliably supplies Haryana's roller flour mills at published reserve prices; 24-crop MSP notification (Dec 2024) at least creates the procurement leg an MSV-style scheme would need.
- **Peer comparison (indicative)**: MP's 2025 industrial policy gives 100% mandi-fee reimbursement for 5 years capped at 50% of P&M investment ([Finraja summary of MP policy](https://finraja.com/mp-policy-2025-for-agri-dairy-and-food-processing/); [MP Horticulture](https://www.mphorticulture.gov.in/en/food-processing-policy)); Punjab's mega-basmati-unit mandi/RDF exemptions have been unstable (basmati market-fee exemption withdrawn Sep 2015) ([Tribune](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/archive/features/small-basmati-exporters-ignored-claims-jakhar-315395)); UP runs a dedicated 2023 food-processing policy ([UP policy PDF](https://invest.up.gov.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/English_Final-Food-processing-policy_160423.pdf)). Haryana's exclusion list (no benefits for rice/dal/flour/oil primary processing) is more restrictive than MP's blanket reimbursement. A rigorous CAG audit specific to Haryana food parks or the 2018 policy was **not found** — flag.

## 5. Design Implications for an MSV-Style Guarantee (Minimum Support Volume)

If Haryana wanted to guarantee procurement **and** guaranteed forward sale to processors, the researched precedents suggest a five-part architecture:

1. **Procurement leg — reuse the 24-crop MSP notification + BBY**: MSP purchase (or Bhavantar top-up) by HAFED/HSWC for a *pre-announced volume cap per crop* (the bajra loss showed uncapped procurement without a disposal plan is fiscally toxic). Pre-registration on e-Kharid ties volumes to verified acreage (the NAFED/NCCF farmer-registration model, 60 lakh farmers, shows this scales).
2. **Forward-sale leg — dual channel**: (a) **transparent e-auctions to processors only** (FCI OMSS/NAFEX template: PAN + FSSAI gating, 10–200 MT lots, weekly cadence, published reserve prices); (b) **long-term supply contracts (1–3 year) with anchor processors** at formula prices (MSP + carrying cost − policy discount), which the OMSS never offers — this is the single biggest gap the ethanol experience exposes, since distilleries invested against a supply promise that was paused in 2023.
3. **Demand creation where demand is thin** — the ethanol lesson: government can conjure processor demand via mandates plus feedstock-differentiated pricing (maize ₹71.86/L vs rice ₹60.32/L steered the industry within one season). State-level analogues: millet mandates in mid-day meals/PDS for bajra offtake; the approved Panipat multi-feed ethanol plant (₹200 cr, 90 KLPD) as captive maize/damaged-grain demand ([ChiniMandi](https://www.chinimandi.com/haryana-approves-rs-200-crore-multi-feed-ethanol-plant-at-panipat-sugar-mill/)).
4. **Obligation-to-buy where processing capacity is captive** — the **sugarcane FRP model**: mills are legally bound (Sugarcane Control Order 1966 under the EC Act) to pay FRP within 14 days, with 15% interest and revenue-recovery attachment for default ([Shankar IAS FRP note](https://www.shankariasparliament.com/current-affairs/hike-in-frp-for-sugarcane); [Civils360](https://civils360.com/fair-and-remunerative-price-frp/)). A state MSV could mirror this for, say, kinnow/guava plants that received capital subsidy: subsidy conditional on minimum offtake from registered FPOs at floor prices. The persistent cane-arrears problem (₹5,800 cr national dues in one year, [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/sugar-mills-owe-rs-5-800-cr-to-cane-farmers-113080600588_1.html)) warns that obligations without processor viability just convert price risk into credit risk.
5. **Perishables — the Operation Greens caution**: for TOP/TOTAL crops the Centre chose *subsidies on transport/storage (50%)* and value-chain grants rather than physical procurement, precisely because state agencies handle perishables badly ([Vajiram overview](https://vajiramandravi.com/current-affairs/operation-green-scheme/); [Drishti](https://www.drishtiias.com/to-the-points/paper3/operation-greens-ttp-1-1); slow implementation critique: [Sanskriti IAS](https://www.sanskritiias.com/current-affairs/operation-green-a-project-in-slow-motion)). An MSV for vegetables should therefore be a **volume-triggered deficiency payment plus contracted processor offtake**, not physical state stocking — consistent with Haryana's own BBY instincts.

**Key risk ledger for MSV (from precedent):** reserve-price-below-MSP arbitrage (OMSS); hidden subsidy when sale price < economic cost (rice-ethanol at ₹2,250 vs ~₹4,000 economic cost); leakage/diversion (MP ethanol-rice scare); quality disputes and schedule slippage (CMR); political flip-flops destroying processor trust (Jul 2023 ethanol pause); and cross-border inflow gaming when only one state procures (Rajasthan bajra flowing into Haryana's Bhavantar).
