# Product diversification: monetise the deficit crops, not just the surplus

*Strategy synthesis from the project's verified findings — trade parity, company margins,
sugarcane valorisation, the byproduct giveaway. July 2026.*

## The core mismatch: the obligation is rice+wheat; the opportunity isn't

India's **public-supply commitment** (NFSA/PDS) is rice and wheat. The state has quietly
let that welfare *obligation* define the entire footprint of its grain business — so it
procures 37% of rice and 23% of wheat, sits on 476 LMT of stock at 2× the buffer norm, and
disposes of it *below economic cost* (₹2,320 ethanol, ₹2,550 OMSS vs ~₹4,000 cost). As a
commercial book, rice and wheat are the **worst** crops the state could pick:

- **Surplus = no pricing power.** You cannot earn a margin selling a commodity you are
  structurally long and the world is also long. Export parity for non-basmati rice sits at
  −12% to +2% of the state's cost — its own stock can't exit without loss.
- **The byproduct is given away.** ~₹7,650 cr/yr of rice bran + husk handed to CMR millers
  for free (see [omss-processing-returns.md](omss-processing-returns.md)).
- **Raw grain trading is a 2–4% business that flips to loss** (Cargill India FY23 −₹113 cr,
  Bunge FY23 loss) — verified in [agri_company_financials.csv](../data/agri_company_financials.csv).

Meanwhile the crops the state barely touches — pulses (procured ~0–14%), oilseeds
(~0–9%), sugarcane byproducts — are precisely where margin and strategic value live.

## The diversification thesis: deficit crops carry import-parity pricing power

The decisive difference is **surplus vs deficit**. India is a net *importer* of pulses and
edible oils, which flips the entire economic logic:

| | Rice / wheat | Pulses / oilseeds |
|---|---|---|
| Trade position | net exporter (surplus) | net importer (deficit) |
| Domestic price vs world | export-parity ceiling | **import-parity floor the state can set with tariffs** |
| State procurement is also… | stock accumulation | **import substitution** — forex saved |
| Pricing power | none (long a glut) | real (deficit + the tariff lever) |
| Addressable market | PDS offtake, fixed | ₹19.5 bn/yr edible-oil import bill; tur/masur/chana imports |

Every tonne of pulses or oilseed the state procures and processes domestically is a tonne
*not imported* — so the "return" is not just the crush margin, it is the import bill
avoided. And unlike rice, the state **controls the price floor via customs duty**: the
parity data shows tur landing 29% below MSP in FY26 precisely *because* imports are
duty-free to Mar-2026, while chana duty went to 10% and yellow peas to 30% to defend the
floor ([trade_price_parity.csv](../data/trade_price_parity.csv)). Duty policy and a
processing arm are one instrument.

## The per-crop monetisation playbook

### Sugarcane — the byproduct-cascade gold standard (copy this everywhere)

Sugarcane is the one crop where India already extracts *every* fraction of value, and it is
the template the grain business should imitate. One cane stalk becomes:

- **Sugar** (the historic core, now a low-margin, MSP-frozen drag — 273.9 LMT 2025-26)
- **Ethanol** — the state *created* this demand by mandate and *throttles* it by allocation
  (289 cr litres / ~34 LMT sugar diverted, ESY 2025-26; 56.1% of cane now crushed via the
  B-heavy route). This is the single most successful non-cereal monetisation in Indian
  agriculture — a policy-manufactured, price-administered offtake market.
- **Bagasse → cogeneration power** (mills sell surplus power to the grid) and increasingly
  **→ compressed bio-gas (CBG)** under SATAT
- **Molasses → potable/industrial alcohol**, **press-mud → bio-compost/CBG feedstock**

The lesson: the margin is in the *cascade*, not the primary product. Sugar alone loses
money (₹16,087 cr of cane arrears); sugar + ethanol + power + CBG is a viable business. The
grain book has the same latent cascade and ignores it (rice bran → oil, husk → power/
biochar, broken rice → ethanol, wheat bran → feed).

### Pulses — dal milling margin + buffer trading + the tariff floor

The state (NAFED/NCCF) already procures and disposes of pulses to dal millers by auction —
but sells them *raw*, capturing none of the dal-processing margin. Monetisation routes:

1. **Own/toll dal milling** and retail under the Bharat brand (the pilot exists) — branded
   staple processing earns 11–12% EBITDA (KRBL/LT Foods proof) vs the ~0% raw auction.
2. **Buffer trading with a purpose**: buy in the domestic glut, hold, release in the
   deficit spike — the counter-cyclical spread the ABCDs earn, *if* paired with the tariff
   (raise duty when releasing, so imports don't cap the upside). The pulses buffer is
   already large; today it is run as price-control, not as a margin book.
3. **Import substitution as the real return**: with tur/urad/masur imports at 12–17 LMT/yr,
   every LMT procured domestically at MSP and milled is forex saved and a farmer covered —
   the double dividend rice can never offer.

Caveat: this only works *behind* a managed tariff. Procure pulses at ₹8,000 MSP while
imports land at ₹5,659 (tur, FY26) and you bleed — which is why the diversification must be
a *procure-process-protect* package, not procurement alone.

### Oilseeds — the ₹19.5 bn import bill is the addressable market

Edible oil is India's largest agri import (~₹19.5 bn/yr, 60%+ import-dependent). This is
the biggest single monetisation prize and the state already has the asset model:

1. **Crush margin capture** — oil + oilmeal. HAFED runs its own mustard oil mills on
   commercial account; the meal byproduct is a feed revenue stream (mirroring the sugarcane
   cascade). AWL's edible-oil business shows the raw crush is thin (3.9%), so the value is
   in **branding the oil** — again the Bharat-brand route.
2. **NMEO-Oilseeds / NMEO-Oil Palm alignment**: procurement-backed oilseed expansion is
   simultaneously farm income, import substitution, and feedstock for a state crush-and-
   brand business. The oil-palm mission (Patanjali/others contract-farming in the NE/AP)
   shows the private template; the state's version keeps the margin public.
3. **The tariff lever again**: crude vs refined duty differential already steers the palm
   trade (collapsed palmolein imports 21→3.4 LMT) — the same lever protects a domestic
   crush book.

## The cross-cutting principle: valorise the byproduct, brand the output

Two rules generalise from every crop above:

1. **Never sell the primary product raw when the byproduct pays.** Sugar mills live on
   ethanol + power, not sugar; rice millers live on bran + husk, not the milling fee; oil
   crushers live on meal. The state currently sells rice raw (gifting ₹7,650 cr of bran)
   and pulses raw (gifting the dal margin). Capturing the cascade is the single biggest
   uncaptured return in the system.
2. **Diversify toward deficit, not surplus.** Pricing power comes from being short, not
   long. The commercial arm should tilt to pulses, oilseeds, and cane-derivatives — where
   procurement doubles as import substitution and the state sets the floor with tariffs —
   and treat rice/wheat as the fixed-cost welfare obligation it is, not a business.

## Recommendations

1. **Split the two mandates institutionally.** Rice/wheat procurement = the PDS welfare
   arm, budgeted as a cost. A separate, ring-fenced **commercial agri arm** (COFCO model,
   per the [trader playbook](agri-trader-playbook.md)) diversifies into pulses/oilseeds/
   cane-derivatives for margin — with no role in MSP or buffer policy.
2. **Monetise every crop through its byproduct cascade**, not its primary product. Auction
   CMR/processing rights *with* the byproduct stream priced in (recover the ₹7,650 cr);
   build/toll dal mills, oil crushers, and rice-bran-oil units under the Bharat brand.
3. **Make procurement of deficit crops an explicit import-substitution programme**, paired
   with the customs-duty lever — procure-process-protect as one package, sized by the
   import bill it displaces, not by PDS need.
4. **Copy sugarcane's cascade into grains**: broken rice → ethanol (already policy-linked),
   bran → oil, husk → power/CBG, wheat bran → feed. The demand-creation and price-
   administration machinery already exists for cane; extend it.
5. **Keep the margin public where the state processes** (HAFED model), or auction the
   offtake transparently where it does not — never nominate a private brand into the
   Tier-2 margin the Bharat brand was built to keep public (the open Patanjali-PDS
   question — see [patanjali-pds-model.md](patanjali-pds-model.md) when complete).

## Caveats

Company margins are latest-FY verified (screener.in/CRISIL); the crush/dal-margin
opportunity is inferred from those comparators, not from a state-run P&L (none exists at
scale beyond HAFED). Import-substitution "returns" assume the tariff floor holds — remove
the duty and the domestic-procurement economics invert (tur FY26 is the live example).
Byproduct valorisation assumes offtake markets (rice-bran-oil, cogen, CBG) absorb the
volume; at MSV scale some would need demand-creation of their own.
