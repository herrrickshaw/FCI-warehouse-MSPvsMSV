# FCI Procurement Trends and the MSP vs MSV Question

**Can government purchase guarantees extend beyond rice and wheat — and should they be price-based or volume-based?**

*Synthesis of a 102-agent verified research run (23/25 claims confirmed by 3-vote adversarial verification, 2 refuted), the companion [Haryana linkages brief](haryana-food-processing-linkages.md), and the datasets in [`data/`](../data/MANIFEST.md). July 2026. Every load-bearing figure below carries its verification vote and source.*

---

## 1. FCI procurement: what the last 15 years actually show

**Volumes.** Wheat: 389.92 LMT (RMS 2020-21) → record 433.44 (2021-22) → heatwave crash to 187.92 (2022-23) → 262.02 → 266.05 → recovery to **300.35 LMT in RMS 2025-26** (25.1 lakh farmers paid). Rice: 602.45 LMT rice-equivalent at the KMS 2020-21 peak, easing to 525.44 by 2023-24, then a **record 832.17 LMT of paddy in KMS 2024-25** (~1.19 crore farmers). *(3-0 votes; DFPD Annual Report 2024-25 Tables 3.12/3.14, PIB PRID 2210211.)*

**Depth.** Procurement has roughly tripled since the mid-1980s (~20 MT → 90+ MT by 2019-20) and deepened as a share of output: **over 40% of rice and over 30% of wheat output** by 2019-20 (vs ~30%/~27% in 2013-14). Our own data series (`production_vs_procurement.csv`) reproduces this independently: rice 38–48% of production procured across 2018-24, wheat 17–40%. *(3-0; RAS 2021, arithmetic cross-checks.)*

**Concentration.** Punjab alone still supplies ~47% of wheat procurement (124.57 of 266.05 LMT, RMS 2024-25). But the geography has genuinely broadened since the 1990s: traditional states' share fell from 80%+ to 64% (paddy) / 75% (wheat) by 2019-20, with MP peaking at 25% of wheat procurement in 2014-15 and Telangana becoming a top paddy state (95.25 LMT in 2020-21, easing to 63.86 by 2023-24). *(3-0.)*

**Stocks.** Central pool stocks are pulling away from need: 298.22 LMT (1 Jan 2023) → 347.06 (2024) → **476.85 LMT (1 Jan 2025)** — 184.11 wheat + 290.92 rice + only 1.82 nutri-cereals — with roughly another ~319 LMT rice-equivalent of unmilled paddy outside that figure. *(2-1 vote — figures verified verbatim, single primary source; DFPD AR 2024-25 Annexure-IV.)* Monthly stock-vs-norm series 2021-24 is in `stocks_vs_norms.csv`.

**Cost.** Food subsidy: ₹2,88,719 cr (FY22) → ₹2,72,502 cr (FY23) → ₹2,11,394 cr (FY24) → ₹1,99,500 cr released FY25 (FCI ₹1,29,089 cr + DCP states ₹70,411 cr); ₹2,03,000 cr allocated FY26. *(3-0 across the series.)*

## 2. Why MSP is de facto a two-crop program

MSPs are announced for ~25 commodities; sustained procurement exists only for rice and wheat, from a few states. KMS 2024-25 coarse-grain procurement was **~11.7 LMT versus 832 LMT of paddy — ~70× smaller**. The verified academic literature (IEG WP420; SAGE Millennial Asia 2025; NSS SAS 2019: 23.5% MSP awareness, 8.8% of agri households sold to agencies) locates the cause in **procurement infrastructure and awareness, not price-setting**: where mandis, agencies, and assured PDS offtake exist, MSP binds; elsewhere it is a notification. *(3-0 across five constituent claims.)*

**Farmer coverage — correcting the famous 6%.** The Shanta Kumar-era "only 6% of farmers benefit" is over *all* agricultural households (NSS 2012-13). Among households that actually **sold** the crop, 13% of paddy sellers and 16% of wheat sellers sold to procurement. Official counts: 22.5 lakh wheat farmers (RMS 2024-25), 1.07 crore paddy farmers (KMS 2023-24) paid directly. *(3-0; note: seasonal counts overlap and cannot be summed.)* A widely cited NITI Aayog "7% of farmers / 10% of output" formulation was **refuted** (1-2) in verification — avoid it.

**Our per-crop evidence** (`production_vs_procurement.csv`, latest full year): rice 38%, wheat 23%, ragi 14.8%, jowar 8.4%, bajra 6.5%, moong 4.7%, gram 1.4%, sunflower 0.7%, groundnut/masur 0.1%, tur/urad/soybean/maize ≈ 0%. The price is announced for 23+ crops; the volume shows up for two.

## 3. The track record of extending support

**Cotton is the cleanest natural experiment** for the MSV idea. CCI's MSP operations: 124.61 lakh bales (2019-20), 99.33 (2020-21), **NIL in both 2021-22 and 2022-23** (market above MSP), 32.84 (2023-24), 99.93 (2024-25, ~₹37,500 cr). A pure price-trigger delivers *zero* guaranteed offtake for years — then a massive fiscal spike in distress years. That is exactly the gap a volume floor targets — but the flip side is that price-triggered support correctly concentrates spending in distress years. *(3-0; data.gov.in Rajya Sabha annexure, PIB.)*

**NAFED/PSS is not the money-pit it's assumed to be.** Verified: NAFED's PSS procurement charges ran under 10% of operation cost versus 12.5–16.5% (rice) and 13–17% (wheat) of economic cost under FCI (2007-12), and PSS benefit-cost ratios beat crop-insurance schemes on fiscal cost recovery. *(3-0; IEG WP379.)* But the stronger claim — PSS B-C ratios above 1 "in most years" — was **refuted** (1-2): treat NAFED efficiency as *relative to FCI*, not absolute. A 100% procurement commitment for tur/urad/masur (to 2028-29) is now official policy — a prospective, crop-limited volume guarantee already in motion.

**Verification gap, flagged honestly:** no claims on PM-AASHA's season-by-season outcomes, MP's Bhavantar/PDPS results, WTO AMS interactions, or US CCC / EU intervention specifics survived the adversarial verification round. Those sections rest on the Haryana brief's sourced-but-unverified material and are marked as open questions (§6). The Haryana brief documents the state-level Bhavantar record: bajra procured with a ₹300/qtl top-up then resold at a loss; 2023 sunflower bought ₹1,600 below MSP triggering agitation — notification ≠ purchase even in the state that notified 24 crops.

## 4. MSP vs MSV: the evaluation

**The core MSV insight is correct** — the binding failure of MSP is quantity, not price. Announcing a price without standing purchase capacity produces the cotton pattern (§3) and the 0–15% procurement shares (§2).

**But a universal MSV is physically infeasible.** Public storage (~98 MT across FCI/states/CWC/co-ops; ~92 MT for the central pool in 2025 — our `storage_capacity.csv` shows 776.59 LMT FCI+hired+state as on 30.11.2024, consistent) caps procurement at roughly **30% of the ~261 MT marketed surplus of 14 major crops (~78 MT)**. Storage, not money, is the first binding constraint on any minimum-purchase-volume commitment; perishables face harder disposal constraints still. *(3-0; IEG WP420, cross-checked against 2025 capacity.)*

**The fiscal arithmetic.** The strongest verified model (IEG WP420): assured MSP for 14 crops via 30%-of-surplus procurement plus deficiency payments on the rest ≈ **₹3.40 lakh crore/year** — an incremental ₹2.31 lakh crore, +95% over the FY22 food subsidy, taking food support from 7% to 15% of agricultural GDP. *(3-0; one credible point in the debated ₹1–10+ lakh crore legal-MSP range — CRISIL/CACP estimates did not survive verification.)*

**The offtake side — where the ethanol caveat comes in.** A volume guarantee is only as good as its disposal channel. The companion brief documents that government *can* manufacture industrial offtake for state-procured grain: FCI rice to distilleries scaled to 52–72 LMT in ESY 2024-25 via OMSS e-auctions; OMC tenders price ethanol by feedstock (maize ₹71.86/L vs FCI-rice ₹60.32/L), steering industry to whichever grain the state wants moved; and the **NAFED/NCCF maize-for-ethanol loop** — MSP procurement from pre-registered farmers, delivery to distilleries at ₹2,291/qtl — is a complete MSV-style circuit already running for a non-PDS crop. The same record shows the conditions: a mandate creating captive demand, administered pricing, tolerance for selling below economic cost (₹2,250 vs ~₹4,000/qtl — a hidden transfer to distilleries), and vulnerability to policy whiplash (the July 2023 pause). OMSS(D) wheat to flour millers (71+ LMT sold in 2023-24) proves the e-auction-to-processors channel at scale; `offtake_by_scheme.csv` carries the series.

**Verdict.** MSV beats MSP-as-notification for storables with existing processing demand (pulses, maize, millets in PDS/ICDS range), provided the volume is **capped and pre-announced** with the disposal channel contracted *before* procurement. MSV loses to deficiency payments for perishables and to income support for everything where the state cannot credibly store or sell. A universal legal guarantee of either kind fails on storage physics before it fails on money.

## 5. Recommended architecture (verified core + Haryana operating detail)

The strongest verified prescription *(3-0, single-source — marked medium)*: **retain rice-wheat procurement, extend the volume guarantee to pulses (where NAFED is relatively efficient and 100% tur/urad/masur commitments already exist), and shift non-staples to income support** — an enhanced PM-KISAN covering half of average cultivation costs ≈ ₹1.22 lakh crore/year, about a third of universal MSP extension. Layered with the Haryana brief's operational lessons:

1. **Capped, pre-registered procurement volumes per crop** (e-Kharid/NAFED-portal registration ties volume to verified acreage; uncapped bajra buying ended in loss-making resale).
2. **Contract the offtake before the purchase**: weekly e-auctions restricted to processors (PAN+FSSAI gating, OMSS template) *plus* 1–3-year formula-priced supply contracts with anchor processors — the instrument OMSS has never offered and whose absence the 2023 ethanol pause exposed.
3. **Create demand where it is thin**: blending-style mandates and feedstock-differentiated pricing moved an entire industry to maize within one season; millet mandates in PDS/mid-day meals are the state-level analogue.
4. **Deficiency payments, not stocks, for perishables** (Operation Greens' own design choice; Haryana's BBY covers 21 horticulture crops).
5. **Price the hidden subsidy openly**: every quintal sold below economic cost is a transfer to processors — budget it explicitly rather than burying it in the FCI balance sheet.

## 6. Open questions the evidence could not settle

- Season-by-season PM-AASHA/PSS/PDPS outcomes and fiscal costs (closest existing analogue to MSV; nothing survived verification).
- WTO AMS/de-minimis interaction of any legal MSP/MSV guarantee (India already invokes the peace clause for rice), and what drives the ₹1–10+ lakh crore estimate spread.
- Current economic-cost-per-quintal vs MSP wedge, and 2025-26 stocks vs operational norms in numeric form.
- How the EU's retreat from open-ended intervention buying (butter/grain mountains) maps onto MSV as a cautionary precedent.
