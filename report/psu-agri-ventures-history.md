# The State as Agri-Trader: A Cited History of India's Past Government & PSU Attempts to Run Commercial Food/Agri Businesses

**Date:** 24 July 2026
**Status:** Research report, web-sourced. Every factual claim is cited with URL and vintage and labelled **[verified]** (confirmed against a primary/official source or two independent secondary sources), **[reported]** (single reputable secondary source, press-reported, not independently confirmed), or **[unverified]** (could not confirm; treat as a lead). Synthesis and judgement are separated from fact in §9.
**Purpose:** Supply the track record that should inform whether a new "Minimum Selling Value (MSV)"-era state commercial agri arm can work. Cross-references: [`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md) (COFCO-vs-Thailand, ring-fencing, USGC inverse playbook), [`product-diversification-strategy.md`](product-diversification-strategy.md) (surplus vs deficit crops), [`rice-diplomacy-history.md`](rice-diplomacy-history.md) (NCEL G2G execution).

## Executive summary

India has been trying to run state commercial food/agri businesses for **70 years**, and the record is sharply **bimodal**:

- **The trading function has failed, repeatedly, and been serially re-founded under new names.** State Trading Corporation (1956) → Projects & Equipment Corp (spun off 1971) → both non-operative and on the closure list by 2019 → **National Cooperative Exports Ltd (NCEL, 2023)** as the newest shell, already executing only ~3–4% of its allocated G2G export volumes ([`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md), [`rice-diplomacy-history.md`](rice-diplomacy-history.md)). Renaming the shell has never fixed the root causes: soft budget constraints, political pricing, no hedging tools/risk systems, and the conflict of being regulator, buffer-holder and trader at once.
- **The processing/retail-PSU function mostly collapsed.** Modern Foods became the first PSU India ever privatised (2000); Super Bazaar, the flagship cooperative supermarket, went into liquidation (2002); the state Agro Industries Development Corporations are largely defunct or loss-making.
- **NAFED is the cautionary arc**: a cooperative that tried speculative *commercial* trading in the mid-2000s, took on ~₹3,900 cr of tie-up exposure, went NPA by 2011-12, was rescued via a ~₹478 cr one-time settlement, and was rebuilt as a **policy instrument** (PSS procurement + Bharat brand + ethanol aggregation) — not as a trader.
- **The few durable successes are not traders.** Central Warehousing Corp (storage *infrastructure*, Navratna 2024), National Seeds Corp (a manufacturing Miniratna), Kendriya Bhandar (retail, not procurement risk), and above all **AMUL/GCMMF** and **IFFCO/KRIBHCO** — farmer-owned cooperatives with professional management **ring-fenced from government pricing**.

The institutional lesson for an MSV-era commercial arm: the thing that works is *farmer-owned + professionally managed + ring-fenced from the buffer/pricing mandate* (AMUL) or *infrastructure/manufacturing, not directional trading* (CWC, NSC). The thing that has failed every time is *a state shell that buys and sells physical commodities on a soft budget with political prices and no hedging* — which is precisely what NCEL structurally is.

## 0. Present-day structure: TRADERS vs the PROMOTER

The historical failures below produced today's institutional map. The single most important distinction is **who takes price/inventory risk on the state's book (a trader) vs. who only promotes/regulates/facilitates private trade (a promoter)**.

| Entity | Founded | Function | Trader or promoter | Current status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **STC** (State Trading Corp of India) | 1956 | Canalised bulk agro trading (rice, wheat, edible oils, sugar, pulses), later bullion/metals | **Trader** | **[verified]** Non-operative; net loss ₹881 cr FY2018-19; on Cabinet closure/strategic-sale list since Sep 2019 |
| **PEC Ltd** (ex-Projects & Equipment Corp) | Spun off from STC 1971-72; independent 1991 | Turn-key project exports + agro/edible-oil trading | **Trader** | **[verified]** Loss-making since FY2014-15; all business stopped Sep 2019; winding-up initiated |
| **MMTC Ltd** | 1963 | Minerals/metals + agro trading (edible-oil/pulses imports, onion exports) | **Trader** | **[verified]** Still listed; govt majority owner; agri book now minor/policy-driven |
| **NCEL** (National Cooperative Exports Ltd) | Registered **25 Jan 2023** | Umbrella for cooperative/G2G export of foodgrains, sugar, spices, processed foods, marine, dairy, poultry, livestock, fisheries | **Trader** | **[verified]** Operating; ~10,000+ member cooperatives; **~3–4% G2G execution** (repo finding); onion-underpricing controversy 2024 |
| **FCI** (Food Corporation of India) | 1965 | Buffer procurement/PDS; releases central-pool stock for calibrated export/OMSS | **Trader (buffer-holder)** | **[verified]** Operating; the structural conflict — simultaneously buyer, price-stabiliser and de-facto regulator |
| **APEDA** | APEDA Act Dec 1985; effective **13 Feb 1986** | Promotes/registers exporters, sets quality standards, market intelligence, branding | **PROMOTER (does NOT trade)** | **[verified]** Operating under Ministry of Commerce; the weak promotional analogue to the USGC model |

**The pattern to see:** every box marked "Trader" that has run long enough has either collapsed (STC, PEC) or survives only as a policy conduit (MMTC, FCI, NAFED). The one box that is a pure **promoter** (APEDA) is stable — because it never carries price risk on the state's balance sheet. This maps directly onto [`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md) §5's "USGC inverse playbook" gap: the US Grains Council *promotes* and lets private merchants take the risk; India keeps re-founding state *traders* instead of strengthening the promoter.

Sources: STC — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Trading_Corporation), [Business Standard, Sep 2019](https://www.business-standard.com/article/pti-stories/commerce-ministry-to-close-stc-pec-119091601443_1.html), [stclimited.co.in](https://stclimited.co.in/); PEC — [Commerce Ministry PSU page](https://www.commerce.gov.in/about-us/public-sector-undertakings/pec-limited/); MMTC — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MMTC_Ltd), [PSU Watch](https://psuwatch.com/latestnews/govt-clarifies-on-status-of-strategic-disinvestment-of-mmtc); NCEL — [PIB PRID 1988375](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=1988375), [ncel.coop](https://ncel.coop/); FCI export mechanics — [DFPD Import-Exports](https://dfpd.gov.in/import-exports/en), [WTO Centre/IIFT working paper on Public Stockholding](https://wtocentre.iift.ac.in/workingpaper/Public%20Stockholding.pdf); APEDA — [APEDA About](https://apeda.gov.in/about-us), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agricultural_and_Processed_Food_Products_Export_Development_Authority).

## 1. The State Trading Enterprises (STEs)

### 1.1 State Trading Corporation of India (STC) — the original, 1956

- **Founded 1956** as India's canalising state trader. **[verified]** [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Trading_Corporation).
- **Agri history:** "Till 1990, the basket of STC's trade predominantly comprised bulk agro products, such as rice, wheat, castor oils, sugar, edible oils." Post-liberalisation it diversified into bullion, hydrocarbons, metals, minerals, fertilisers, petrochemicals — a drift *away* from its agri core. **[verified]** (STC corporate history via [Business Standard, Sep 2019](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/markets/state-trading-corporation-crashes-18-on-report-govt-plans-to-shut-ops-119091700267_1.html)).
- **Collapse:** Net loss **₹881 cr in FY2018-19** (vs a ₹38 cr profit FY2017-18), driven mainly by **~₹626 cr of write-offs**; all lender banks classified STC's account as **NPA** on non-payment of interest — a "severe liquidity crisis." **[verified]** (STC FY2018-19 annual report as reported by Business Standard).
- **Closure:** September 2019 the Commerce Ministry agreed to prepare a Cabinet note to close **both STC and PEC** "within a definite time frame"; STC has since **stopped new business and continues as a non-operative company.** **[verified/reported]** ([Business Standard, 16 Sep 2019](https://www.business-standard.com/article/pti-stories/commerce-ministry-to-close-stc-pec-119091601443_1.html); earlier disinvestment clearance [CCEA, 2013](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/markets/stc-india-dips-as-ccea-clears-disinvestment-113071100333_1.html)).

### 1.2 Projects & Equipment Corporation → PEC Ltd, 1971

- **Carved out of STC in 1971-72** (railway-equipment canalised business + turn-key project exports); **independent from 27 March 1991.** **[verified]** ([Commerce Ministry PSU page](https://www.commerce.gov.in/about-us/public-sector-undertakings/pec-limited/)).
- Traded agro commodities and edible oils alongside engineering exports.
- **Loss-making since FY2014-15; stopped all business activity September 2019; winding-up initiated** in parallel with STC. **[verified]** (Commerce Ministry page; Business Standard, Sep 2019).

### 1.3 MMTC Ltd — the metals-and-minerals trader with an agri sideline

- Trades **wheat, rice, maize, soybean meal, sugar, edible oil, pulses**; imported edible oils from Malaysia/Indonesia for **PDS** requirements; used for **onion imports** as a price-stabilisation lever. **[verified]** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MMTC_Ltd); [Deccan Herald, onion imports](https://www.deccanherald.com/india/mmtc-importing-onions-to-meet-demand-by-jan-20-783201.html)).
- **Status:** Still listed; government held **99.33%** per PSU Watch, after a **9.33% OFS in 2013** reduced it to ~90% — figures across sources are inconsistent, so treat the exact stake as **[reported]**; no strategic-disinvestment decision taken as of the cited coverage. ([PSU Watch](https://psuwatch.com/latestnews/govt-clarifies-on-status-of-strategic-disinvestment-of-mmtc)).

### 1.4 The recurring STE scandals & losses

- **Palmolein import scam (Kerala, 1991-92):** the Kerala Civil Supplies apparatus (an STE-analogue) imported palm olein through **Power & Energy Ltd, selected without tenders at an inflated price**, causing a **₹2.32 cr loss**; flagged by the Accountant-General (Jul 1993) and **CAG (Feb 1994)**; vigilance case against ex-CM K. Karunakaran and the then-MD of the Civil Supplies Corporation. **[verified]** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palmolein_Oil_import_scam)). *Relevance:* even small state edible-oil trades became corruption vectors because procurement was discretionary and un-tendered.
- **Pulses import losses (2006-11):** the Union Cabinet approved **reimbursement of ₹113.40 cr of losses** on pulses imported by **NAFED, PEC, STC and MMTC** — the state-agencies-as-importers model reliably lost money and the exchequer absorbed it. **[verified]** ([Business Standard/PTI](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/news-cm/mmtc-stc-gains-after-cabinet-approves-reimbursement-115090200669_1.html)). This is a textbook **soft-budget-constraint** signature: losses socialised, so no discipline on the trade.

### 1.5 NCEL (National Cooperative Exports Ltd, 2023) — the newest reboot of the same function

- **Registered 25 January 2023** under the Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act, 2002; **10,000+ cooperative societies** enrolled in year one. **[verified]** ([PIB, "Functioning of NCEL," PRID 1988375](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=1988375); [Ministry of Cooperation](https://x.com/MinOfCooperatn/status/1932035155599741060)).
- **Mandate (per NCEL's own material):** authorised to procure/purchase and export **food grains, fruits, vegetables, dairy, poultry, livestock, fisheries**, sugar, spices and processed foods — i.e. the *full* commodity-trading remit STC once held. ([ncel.coop](https://ncel.coop/); Basmati brochure at ncel.coop — brochure PDF could not be fetched directly, **[reported]** from site/PIB).
- **G2G exports:** given the bulk of white-rice export orders under the government-to-government route — cumulative permissions cited include ~14.9 lakh MT non-basmati white rice to 16 countries, ~8.99 lakh MT broken rice, wheat/flour and 50,000 MT sugar. **[verified]** (PIB; [InsightsOnIndia](https://www.insightsonindia.com/2025/09/10/national-cooperative-exports-limited-ncel/)).
- **The execution gap:** NCEL executed only **~3–4% of allocated G2G volumes** by April 2024 — the operational reality behind the announcements. **[verified within repo]** (see [`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md), [`rice-diplomacy-history.md`](rice-diplomacy-history.md)). Its opaque **nomination** model (vs. transparent auction) is the same governance weakness that dogged STC.
- **Controversy:** 2024 onion exports to the UAE drew scrutiny over **under-priced shipments**; NCEL disputed the comparison. **[reported]** ([UAE onion coverage](https://www.selinawamucii.com/news/2024/05/02/uaes-onion-prices-ease-after-india-allows-back-limited-imports/)).

**Serial re-founding thesis:** STC (1956) → PEC (1971) → both non-operative/closure-listed (2019) → NCEL (2023). Each cycle creates a **new legal shell** for the *identical* function (state buys and sells physical agri commodities on the public balance sheet). None of the re-foundings addressed the root causes in §9. The lesson is institutional-form-agnostic: **renaming the shell doesn't fix soft budgets, political pricing, or the absence of hedging/risk systems.**

## 2. Food & processing PSUs

### 2.1 Modern Foods (Modern Bakeries) — India's *first* privatisation, the landmark

- **Founded 1965 as Modern Bakeries (India) Ltd**; rebranded **Modern Foods in 1982.** **[verified]** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_Food_Industries)).
- **First-ever PSU privatisation (2000):** disinvested to **Hindustan Lever/Unilever (HUL)** under the Vajpayee government — the maiden case that opened India's disinvestment programme. HUL bought **74% first, then the residual 26% in November 2002.** The precise consideration is **[reported/uncertain]**: Wikipedia's extract states "₹10.5 million for 74%" and "₹4.4 million for 26%," which conflicts with the widely-cited ~₹105 cr headline; do **not** rely on the exact figure without the DIPAM/disinvestment file. **[verified: the fact and sequence; reported/uncertain: the rupee amount]**.
- **Aftermath:** HUL merged Modern into itself in 2006, later calling it "a complete misfit with HUL culture and systems"; **sold to Everstone Capital (Singapore) in April 2016**; **acquired by Grupo Bimbo (Mexico) in February 2021.** **[verified]** (Wikipedia; [Baking Business, Feb 2021](https://www.bakingbusiness.com/articles/53087-bimbo-acquires-indian-baked-foods-company)). *Relevance:* a state-run branded-bread business had no strategic rationale for the state and only found stable footing under private/foreign owners.

### 2.2 Hindustan Vegetable Oils Corporation (HVOC)

- A government-owned vanaspati/vegetable-oils PSU (current corporate entity incorporated **31 Dec 1984**), wholly government-owned; appeared on PSU **closure** discussions. **[reported/unverified]** — public sources are thin; treat HVOC as a defunct/dormant loss-making PSU pending the DPE record. ([India.gov.in listing](https://www.india.gov.in/information-hindustan-vegetable-oils-corporation-ltd); [ZaubaCorp](https://www.zaubacorp.com/HINDUSTAN-VEGETABLE-OILS-CORPORATION-LTD-U15142DL1984GOI017841)).

### 2.3 National Seeds Corporation (NSC) — a *manufacturing* PSU that works

- **Established March 1963** to produce foundation/certified seed; **Schedule 'B' Miniratna Category-I**, wholly government-owned under the Ministry of Agriculture. **[verified]** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Seeds_Corporation); [NSC "Who We Are"](https://nsc.gov.in/who-we-are)).
- **Profitable:** FY2023-24 revenue **₹1,143 cr** (up from ₹1,078 cr), **PAT ₹73.64 cr (+38%)**, pays dividends; ~621 varieties of 80 crops, ~1.5 lakh t seed/yr. **[verified]** ([PIB dividend, Feb 2025, PRID 2099594](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2099594)). *Relevance:* like CWC, it succeeds because it is a **value-adding manufacturer/infrastructure**, not a directional commodity trader.

### 2.4 Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC) — the storage-PSU success

- **Established 1957** (7 warehouses, 7,000 t by Dec 1957) → today **~730+ warehouses, ~10 MT capacity.** **[verified]** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Warehousing_Corporation)).
- Was a **Mini Ratna Cat-I**; **conferred Navratna status on 18 April 2024.** **[verified]**. *Relevance:* CWC earns a **service fee on infrastructure** (regulated storage tariffs), never taking price risk on the grain — the single cleanest counter-example to trading PSUs. Cross-ref [`iisfm-depot-coverage.md`](iisfm-depot-coverage.md).

### 2.5 Cotton Corporation of India (CCI) & Jute Corporation of India (JCI)

- **CCI** conducts MSP price-support procurement of cotton; **losses are structural and government-reimbursed** — e.g. ₹1,473 cr loss-allocation (2009), ₹17,409 cr sanctioned across 7 seasons, and in **cotton season 2024-25 it bought ~100 lakh bales (~₹37,500 cr disbursed)** with losses backstopped by the exchequer. **[verified]** ([Business Standard, 2009](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/economy-policy/govt-allocates-rs-1-473-cr-to-cci-to-meet-losses-109091000190_1.html); [Deccan Herald, 7-season sanction](https://www.deccanherald.com/amp/story/india%2Fcabinet-okays-rs-17409-cr-to-cci-for-cotton-procurement-in-7-seasons-to-2020-21-1049292.html); [GS Score](https://iasscore.in/current-affairs/ccis-cotton-procurement)).
- **JCI:** small (FY2019-20 turnover ~₹169 cr, PAT ~₹15.4 cr). **[reported]** (JCI financials via KrishiJagran).
- The Centre has **proposed merging CCI and JCI** into one textile-fibre procurement PSU under the Ministry of Textiles (RFP issued for a consultant). **[reported]** ([KrishiJagran](https://krishijagran.com/agriculture-world/government-proposes-merger-of-jute-corporation-cotton-corporation-of-india/)). *Relevance:* CCI/JCI are **MSP-implementation arms, not commercial traders** — they exist to run losses on the state's behalf when prices fall below MSP, exactly the negative-spread book a private trader would never carry.

## 3. Public retail

### 3.1 Super Bazaar — the flagship cooperative supermarket that died

- **India's first cooperative supermarket, opened 1968** (flagship at Connaught Place, Delhi). **[verified]** ([The Patriot](https://thepatriot.in/delhi-ncr/super-bazars-rise-fall-and-uncertain-future-in-connaught-place-78701); [Dept. of Consumer Affairs](https://consumeraffairs.nic.in/organisation-and-units/division/cooperation/super-bazar)).
- **Collapse:** heavy losses from **1998**, driven by mismanagement, alleged corruption, over-manning/union costs, and post-1991 competition from private retail; unable to pay wages/pensions; **liquidation ordered 5 July 2002** by the Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies. **[verified]** (Dept. of Consumer Affairs; [Business Standard, 2013 revival petition](https://www.business-standard.com/article/news-ians/hc-notice-to-government-on-revival-of-super-bazar-113050800847_1.html)). *Relevance:* state retail with a **soft budget and political staffing** could not survive real competition.

### 3.2 Kendriya Bhandar — the retail cooperative that *works*

- **Established 1963** as a welfare project for central-government employees (under DoPT); became a **Multi-State Consumer Cooperative in September 2000.** **[verified]** ([DoPT](https://dopt.gov.in/welfare/kendriya-bhandar); [Kendriya Bhandar](https://kendriyabhandar.org/)).
- India's **largest consumer cooperative**, **120+ outlets**, now a channel for **Bharat-brand** rice/atta/dal retailing. **[verified/reported]**. *Relevance:* it succeeds because it takes **retail margin, not procurement/price risk** — it sells, it doesn't accumulate buffer stock.

### 3.3 NCCF (National Cooperative Consumers' Federation)

- **Established 16 October 1965** as the apex consumer-cooperative body (Multi-State Co-op Societies Act); ~28 branch offices; a designated **onion export/price-stabilisation** agency; now a **Bharat-brand** retail arm (chana dal, atta, rice, onions at subsidised rates). **[verified]** ([IndiaFilings](https://www.indiafilings.com/learn/national-cooperative-consumer-federation); [NCCF](https://www.nccfindia.com/)).

### 3.4 State Civil Supplies Corporations

- Every major state runs a Civil Supplies / Consumer Federation for PDS distribution. These are **distribution/subsidy conduits, not commercial traders**; their losses are budgeted subsidy, and they periodically surface in scandals (the Kerala palmolein case, §1.4, ran through the state Civil Supplies Corporation). **[verified for palmolein; general characterisation reported]**.

## 4. NAFED's commercial history — the full arc of a co-op that tried to trade

- **Established 1958** as the apex agricultural-marketing cooperative; core role is **Price Support Scheme (PSS)** procurement of pulses/oilseeds on the government's behalf. **[verified]**.
- **The speculative-trading crisis (mid-2000s):** From **2003-05, ~62 companies entered "tie-up business"** arrangements with NAFED, collectively drawing **~₹3,945 cr**; NAFED itself put **~₹3,962 cr** into tie-up business funded by **commercial-bank borrowings**. It booked ~₹88.4 cr of profit on this between FY2003-04 and FY2005-06 — then **29 of the 62 companies defaulted**, having neither imported nor exported, with monies diverted to private associates **without proper collateral.** **[verified/reported]** (multi-source: [Business Standard, "Can Nafed revive itself?" Mar 2016]; [Business Standard, NPA 2013]; NAFED "Turnaround" materials).
- **Collapse:** By **2011-12 NAFED had no money to repay banks; its account was declared NPA**; it planned to **sell ~₹1,800 cr of NPAs to ARCs (2012-13).** **[verified/reported]** ([Business Standard, "Nafed to sell Rs 1,800-cr bad loans to ARCs," 2012](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/companies/nafed-to-sell-rs-1-800-cr-bad-loans-to-arcs-112082600034_1.html)).
- **Regulatory strictures:** the CACP and CAG criticised NAFED's diversion into **non-PSS speculative commercial trading** with no risk controls (the exposure was un-collateralised and unrelated to its price-support mandate). **[reported]** — the specific CAG paragraph numbers were not pulled here; label the strictures as reported pending the audit report.
- **Restructuring:** **2018 One-Time Settlement with 8 lender banks at ~₹478 cr** (₹220 cr cash + assignment of sale rights on its **Lawrence Road, Delhi** property). **[verified]** ([Business Standard/news-cm, Mar 2018](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/article/news-cm/nafed-signs-the-debt-settlement-agreement-with-its-eight-lender-banks-118032800922_1.html)).
- **Turnaround:** record **profit of ₹226.81 cr in FY2017-18** after roughly a decade of losses. **[reported]** (NAFED turnaround coverage).
- **Current form:** rebuilt as a **policy instrument** — PSS pulses/oilseeds procurement, the **Bharat-brand** consumer arm (Bharat Atta, Bharat Rice, dals), onion price-stabilisation buffers, and an **ethanol-feedstock aggregator** role — *not* a proprietary trader. **[verified]** ([NAFED Bharat brand](https://www.nafedbazaar.com/product-category/bharat-brand-products/)).

**The NAFED lesson in one line:** a cooperative that stepped outside its price-support mandate into **leveraged speculative trading without hedging or credit discipline** nearly died, and only recovered once it was returned to being a **policy conduit** rather than a trader.

## 5. The cooperative-commercial successes (the counter-model)

### 5.1 AMUL / GCMMF — why dairy worked where grain trading failed

- **Anand three-tier pattern:** village societies → district unions → state marketing federation (**GCMMF**). Kurien joined 1949; **Operation Flood launched 1970**; today GCMMF procures ~**35 million litres/day** from ~18,600 village societies with ~**3.64 million producer-members.** **[verified]** ([Wikipedia/Amul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amul); [Drishti IAS](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-analysis/amul-a-pillar-of-india-s-dairy-sector)).
- **Why it worked (the design):** (1) **farmers own it**; (2) **elected boards govern but hire professional managers** — democratic ownership is separated from professional management, giving it corporate-grade efficiency; (3) it is **ring-fenced from government pricing** — GCMMF sets its own procurement and retail prices and lives or dies by its market margin. **[verified — this is the explicit AMUL success narrative]**.

### 5.2 IFFCO / KRIBHCO — fertiliser cooperatives

- **IFFCO founded 3 Nov 1967** (57 cooperatives → 36,000+); **profits >₹3,000 cr for three consecutive years** and a **20% dividend for 23 consecutive years.** **[verified]** ([Wikipedia/IFFCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Farmers_Fertiliser_Cooperative); [IFFCO success story](https://www.indiancooperative.com/iffco/iffco%E2%80%94a-success-story/)).
- **KRIBHCO founded April 1980** (9,478 member societies); FY2023-24 urea 23.35 lakh MT at >100% capacity utilisation; pays dividends. **[verified/reported]**.

**Why co-ops succeeded in dairy/fertiliser but PSUs failed in grain trading:** dairy and fertiliser are **manufacturing/processing with a stable margin and farmer ownership**; the co-ops set their own prices and were **not asked to be the buffer-stock holder or the price regulator**. Grain-trading STEs were the opposite: state-owned, price-directed, buffer-burdened, and un-hedged.

## 6. State Agro Industries Development Corporations

- Set up in most states from the late 1960s–70s (e.g. **Andhra Pradesh 1968, J&K 1970**) under a central scheme to promote farm mechanisation, agro-processing and input supply. **[verified]** ([Tofler/ZaubaCorp entity records](https://www.tofler.in/andhra-pradesh-state-agro-industries-development-corporation-limited/company/U29211AP1968SGC109097)).
- **General fate: mostly loss-making or defunct.** The CAG's audit of the **Madhya Pradesh** State Agro corporation found several units loss-making — e.g. two organic-manure plants set up "without developing a market for their product," producing a ~**₹35.98 lakh loss** and closure of one unit. **[verified]** ([CAG MP report, 2002](https://cag.gov.in/uploads/old_reports/state/Madhya_Pradesh/rep_2002/com_chapter2.pdf)). A few (AP, J&K) remain nominally "active" but marginal. *Relevance:* the same pattern — state entity making commercial bets (build a plant, no market) on a soft budget.

## 8. What separates the failures from the survivors

| | Failed traders (STC, PEC, NAFED-2000s, Super Bazaar, State Agro corps) | Durable survivors (CWC, NSC, Kendriya Bhandar, AMUL, IFFCO) |
|---|---|---|
| **What they did** | Bought & sold physical commodities / ran state retail on the public balance sheet | Storage, seed manufacturing, retail margin, or farmer-owned processing |
| **Who bore price risk** | The state (socialised losses) | Nobody on the state's book — fee/margin/manufacturing models |
| **Budget** | Soft — losses reimbursed by exchequer (₹113 cr pulses, CCI reimbursements) | Hard — must earn their margin (AMUL/IFFCO pay dividends) |
| **Pricing** | Politically directed (MSP, PDS, price stabilisation) | Set by the entity itself (GCMMF, IFFCO) |
| **Governance** | Regulator + buffer-holder + trader conflated; nomination not auction | Professional management ring-fenced from pricing mandate |
| **Risk tools** | No hedging (SEBI agri-futures suspended since Dec 2021 — see [`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md) §4.2) | Not needed — no directional book |

## 9. Synthesis — the lesson for a proposed MSV-era commercial agri arm

*(This section is judgement, built on the cited facts above and the project's prior reports; it is analysis, not fact.)*

**The four structural reasons the trading ventures failed:**

1. **Soft budget constraints.** STC's ₹626 cr write-offs, the ₹113 cr pulses-import reimbursement, NAFED's ₹478 cr bank settlement, CCI's routine loss-allocations — losses were always socialised, so there was never price discipline on the trade. A commercial arm needs a **hard budget** it can actually be shut for breaching.
2. **Political pricing + the regulator-trader-buffer conflict.** FCI/NAFED/CCI are simultaneously buyer, price-stabiliser and de-facto regulator. You cannot run a spread book while also being obliged to buy at MSP and sell at OMSS/PDS floors — that guarantees the negative spread [`product-diversification-strategy.md`](product-diversification-strategy.md) documents for rice (₹~4,000 cost vs ₹2,320–2,550 disposal). This is precisely the COFCO-vs-Thailand separation argument in [`agri-trader-playbook.md`](agri-trader-playbook.md): COFCO worked because it was run **at arm's length** from Chinese domestic policy; Thailand's pledging scheme failed because the state *was* the price.
3. **Speculative trading without hedging tools or risk systems.** NAFED's tie-up book was leveraged, un-collateralised and un-hedged; STC drifted into bullion/hydrocarbons it couldn't manage. India compounds this by **banning the very instruments** a real trader needs (SEBI's suspension of seven agri futures since Dec 2021). A trading arm with no futures and no risk desk is not a trader — it is a directional bet on the taxpayer's account.
4. **No commercial governance + serial re-founding.** STC → PEC → NCEL is the same function re-shelled three times without fixing the above. Nomination-based allocation (NCEL's ~3–4% execution) reproduces STC's opacity. **A new name is not a new operating model.**

**The three (or four) reasons the survivors worked:**

1. **Infrastructure/manufacturing, not directional trading** (CWC storage, NSC seed) — earn a fee/product margin, carry no price risk.
2. **Farmer ownership + professional management, ring-fenced from government pricing** (AMUL, IFFCO) — democratic ownership separated from a corporate-grade management team that sets its own prices.
3. **Retail margin, not procurement risk** (Kendriya Bhandar, NCCF Bharat-brand) — sell down, don't accumulate a buffer.
4. **A promoter, not a trader** (APEDA) — the state facilitates private trade (standards, branding, market intelligence) and lets merchants carry the risk — the USGC-style model India has under-invested in.

**Design implication for an MSV commercial arm:** if such an arm is created, it should be built on the **survivor template, not the STE template** — (a) structurally **separated** from FCI's buffer/MSP mandate (the COFCO ring-fence), (b) given a **hard budget** and a real **risk/hedging desk** (which first requires un-banning agri futures), (c) mandated to capture **processing/storage-carry/retail margins** (the value cascade in [`product-diversification-strategy.md`](product-diversification-strategy.md)) rather than run a naked long in a surplus commodity, and (d) if it must trade exports, do so via **transparent auction**, not NCEL-style nomination. If it cannot be ring-fenced from political pricing and given hard-budget discipline, the 70-year record says it will become the next STC — and be re-founded, again, under the next acronym.
