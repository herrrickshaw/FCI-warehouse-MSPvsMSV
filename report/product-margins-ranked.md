# Product margins, ranked: real DPR / government-feasibility financials by product

*Net-profit-margin ranking of the food-processing and byproduct products in the cascade thesis,
each anchored to a real Detailed Project Report (DPR) or government feasibility source where one
exists, and labelled **verified** (a DPR / government model report gives the number),
**reported** (a private market-research house or industry source), or **estimated** (analyst
inference where no plant-level figure exists). Data: [`data/product_margins_dpr.csv`](../data/product_margins_dpr.csv).
Anchor benchmark: the 1-TPH cooperative rice mill in [rice-mill-business-model.md](rice-mill-business-model.md)

## The two-number problem: margin-on-sales ≠ return-on-capital

The single most important finding before reading the ranking: **for the milling businesses, the
net profit margin on sales and the IRR point in opposite directions.** A dal mill earns only
~5% net on sales but returns **30% IRR**, because it is a low-capital, high-turnover business —
the ₹1.8 cr plant spins ₹46 cr of grain through itself a year. A nutraceutical extraction earns
40%+ gross margin but on a tonnage so small the *rupees* are trivial. So the table below sorts by
**net profit margin** (as requested), but the **IRR column is the better bankability signal for
the high-volume staples** — read both. The DPRs themselves make this explicit: Rajasthan's dal /
besan / flour DPRs report 19–31% IRR on 2–5% net margins.

## Per-product source detail (the verified DPR figures)

**Rice mill — 1 TPH cooperative (ANCHOR, verified).** NCDC/CSISAC DPR, Chhattisgarh, ~2018:
₹80 lakh project cost, **19% IRR**, DSCR 2.56, payback 5 yr 2 mo, profit on sales **8.5% (Yr II)
→ 15.4% (Yr I)** ramping with utilisation, break-even 63–80%. Custom-milling commission ₹35/qtl.
[rice-mill-business-model.md](rice-mill-business-model.md).

**Dal mill (verified).** Rajasthan RACP Technology DPR (Grant Thornton, ~2018) runs three
profiles: small unit **IRR 31.5%**, RoCE 21.2%, payback 3 yr 11 mo; medium 20-MT/day unit (₹1.8 cr)
**IRR 30%**, RoCE 30.6%, payback 2 yr 10 mo, net margin **~5.3%** (PAT ₹344.7 L on sales ₹6,485 L);
large unit **IRR 31%**, RoCE 26.2%, payback 4 yr 2 mo. Corroborated by the NIFTEM PMFME dal DPR
(improved 480-MT/annum unit: **IRR 25.04%**, BCR 1.03, DSCR 1.54). The pattern is unmistakable —
**thin margin, fat IRR.** [Rajasthan RACP Dal Mill DPR](https://www.tourism.rajasthan.gov.in/content/dam/agriculture/Rajasthan%20Agricultural%20Competitiveness%20Project/ABPFTechDPR/RACP_ABPF_Tech%20DPR_Dal%20Mill.pdf) · [NIFTEM PMFME dal DPR](https://niftem.ac.in/newsite/pmfme/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/daldpr.pdf).

**Roller flour mill / atta-maida-suji (verified).** Rajasthan RACP DPR: **IRR 20–29%**, RoCE 20–30%,
payback 2 yr 9 mo – 3 yr 4 mo, net margin **~3–5.5%** (PAT ₹0.83 L on ₹281 L Yr-1 → 5.5% by Yr-5).
[Rajasthan RACP Wheat Flour Mill DPR](https://www.tourism.rajasthan.gov.in/content/dam/agriculture/Rajasthan%20Agricultural%20Competitiveness%20Project/ABPFTechDPR/RACP_ABPF_Tech%20DPR_Wheat%20flour%20Mill.pdf).

**Besan / gram flour (verified).** Rajasthan RACP DPR: **IRR 19–21%**, RoCE 20%, payback ~3 yr 2 mo,
net margin **~1.5–3.2%** (PAT ₹5.8 L on sales ₹398 L → 3.2% at scale). The thinnest-margin staple —
but still a ~20% IRR because chana turns fast. [Rajasthan RACP Besan Mill DPR](https://www.tourism.rajasthan.gov.in/content/dam/agriculture/Rajasthan%20Agricultural%20Competitiveness%20Project/ABPFTechDPR/RACP_ABPF_Tech%20DPR_Besan%20Mill.pdf).

**Poha / murmura (verified — the standout value-add).** NIFTEM-Thanjavur PMFME model DPR,
1-TPD unit, ₹21.52 lakh project cost: gross profit **14–23%**, PAT rising ₹10.3 L (Yr 2) →
₹14.9 L, net margin **~10–15%**, BCR 1.11, DSCR 5.32, payback **2 yr 10 mo**. The flattened/puffed
value-add roughly **doubles the net margin of plain rice milling** on a tiny ₹21-lakh capex —
the clearest DPR-backed case that the FMCG step pays. [NIFTEM-T PMFME puffed & flaked rice DPR](https://niftem-t.ac.in/pmfme/dpr-price.pdf).

**Mustard oil expeller (verified).** NIFTEM-T PMFME DPR, 110,000 L/annum, ₹15.07 lakh: gross
**14–16%**, net margin **~2–6%** (PAT ₹1.4 L Yr-1 → ₹7.67 L Yr-5 on sales ₹68–123 L), DSCR 3.63.
Small ghani/expeller economics — thin, as edible oil always is. [NIFTEM-T PMFME mustard oil DPR](https://niftem-t.ac.in/pmfme/dpr-moil.pdf).

**Cattle / poultry feed (verified).** Meghalaya AH&Vety Model DPR, 12,000 MT/yr, revenue
₹1,248 L at 100%: **Net Profit Ratio 1.75% → 5.24%**, **Project IRR 19%**, DSCR 2.16. Confirms the
thesis that feed is the low-margin floor of the tree. [Meghalaya Model DPR — Poultry & Cattle Feed](https://megahvt.gov.in/policy/ModelDPRPoultryandCattleFeed.pdf).

**Rice bran oil — solvent extraction (reported, DPR-supported).** IMARC RBO-plant DPR and EIRI's
50-TPD crude-RBO DPR: gross **20–30%**, net **8–15%**; small-plant reports quote rate-of-return
40–78% at 40–50 TPD (economies of scale). This is the extraction-plant figure; a stand-alone
branded **refinery** compresses to 2–4% net (row 17). The repo's own RBO economics
([rice-mill-business-model.md](rice-mill-business-model.md)) show the DORB feed credit is what keeps
the oil margin positive. [IMARC RBO plant DPR](https://www.imarcgroup.com/rice-bran-oil-processing-plant-project-report).

**Cottonseed oil (reported).** IMARC DPR: gross 20–30%, net 8–15% at 20–100 kMT/yr — same edible-oil
band as RBO extraction. [IMARC cottonseed-oil DPR](https://www.imarcgroup.com/cottonseed-oil-processing-plant-project-report).

**Maize wet-milling & wheat gluten+starch (reported).** Integrated maize starch–glucose–sorbitol
plants: **IRR 22–28%, payback 4–5 yr**, net 12–20% (gross 25–35%) per NPCS/EntrepreneurIndia and
IMARC starch benchmarks — but capex ₹80–120 cr, a different league from the ₹20-lakh PMFME units.
Wheat gluten+starch shares the starch-plant 12–20% net band and adds the ~13,000 t/yr gluten
import-substitution angle. [EntrepreneurIndia maize](https://www.entrepreneurindia.co/blogs/maize-processing-business-india/) · [IMARC wheat starch](https://www.imarcgroup.com/wheat-starch-manufacturing-plant-project-report).

**FRK plant (estimated).** No clean government DPR margin; Syndicated Analytics / IMARC plant
reports and a Scribd 200-kg/hr FRK DPR imply healthy scale-up margins (~8–15%), but pricing is
**PDS-tender-competed**, so the realised margin is policy-set, not market-set. Strategic value is
the captive ₹2,700 cr/yr program, not the per-kg spread. [IMARC fortified-rice DPR](https://www.imarcgroup.com/fortified-rice-manufacturing-plant-project-report).

**Rice husk briquette (reported).** MSME guides + IMARC biomass-briquette DPR: gross 25–35%,
net 10–15%, payback ~2 yr, small ₹6–10 lakh entry. [IMARC biomass-briquette DPR](https://www.imarcgroup.com/biomass-briquettes-manufacturing-plant-project-report).

**Precipitated silica from husk ash / nutraceuticals / protein isolates (estimated–reported).**
No co-op-scale DPR exists for any of these. Silica is the biggest *value-density* jump in the tree
(RHA ₹1–3.5/kg → tyre/solar-grade silica) but there is no bankable Indian plant P&L to cite;
nutraceuticals (oryzanol/tocotrienols/vit-E) carry 40–60% gross margins on trivial tonnage;
pea/pulse protein isolate quotes >60% gross conversion margin. All labelled estimated/reported —
real ₹/kg, no DPR backing. [byproduct-product-tree.md](byproduct-product-tree.md).

## Analysis: where the real, bankable margin is

**1. The genuinely high-margin *and* DPR-backed cluster is small — and it is the value-add rice
FMCG + RBO extraction, not the exotic tail.** Filtering the ranking to rows that are BOTH
high-margin (≥10% net) AND have real government/DPR backing leaves essentially four:

- **Poha / murmura (10–15% net, NIFTEM-T PMFME DPR, verified)** — the cleanest win: doubles rice-mill
  margin on ₹21 lakh capex, payback under 3 years.
- **Rice bran oil solvent extraction (8–15% net, IMARC/EIRI DPR)** — the volume anchor of the whole
  savings table (~₹7,500 cr import substitution) and the deepest offtake.
- **Rice mill itself (8.5–15% net, 19% IRR, NCDC DPR)** — the anchor; the platform every other rice
  stream bolts onto.
- **Maize wet-milling / rice-husk briquette (12–20% / 10–15% net, industry DPRs)** — bankable but
  either capital-heavy (maize, ₹80–120 cr) or small (briquette).

**2. The nutraceutical/silica "tail" is high-margin but NOT bankable — no DPR exists.** Oryzanol,
tocotrienols, vitamin-E, precipitated silica, protein isolates all show 20–60% gross margins, but
every one is labelled *estimated/reported* because there is **no Indian plant-level DPR at
cooperative scale**. They are a margin garnish on tiny tonnage — real ₹/kg, immaterial ₹ total —
exactly as [byproduct-product-tree.md](byproduct-product-tree.md) concluded. Don't build the
policy case on them.

**3. Basic milling (dal, flour, besan) is low-margin but the IRR is excellent — the DPRs prove it.**
Dal 5% net / **30% IRR**, flour 4.5% net / **22% IRR**, besan 2.5% net / **20% IRR**. These are the
load-bearing *volume* of the state's grain book, and the government DPRs (Rajasthan RACP, NIFTEM
PMFME) rate them techno-economically viable precisely because low capital + fast turnover beats fat
margin. The "process, don't sell raw" case for pulses rests on IRR, not on margin.

**4. Feed and refining are the floor — confirmed at ~2–4% net by two independent DPRs.** The
Meghalaya feed DPR (1.75–5.24% net, 19% IRR) and branded edible-oil refiners (2–4% net) bracket the
bottom. Feed still clears a 19% IRR on turnover, but it is a floor product — monetise the byproduct,
don't expect it to carry the tree.

**Reconciliation with [consolidated-savings-by-crop.md](consolidated-savings-by-crop.md):** the
savings table's four-tier read is confirmed at the plant level. Its "edible-oil block dominates" =
RBO extraction's 8–15% net on the largest volume. Its "pulses dal-processing margin the state gifts"
= the dal DPR's 30% IRR (the *return* the state forgoes by auctioning raw, even though the *margin*
is thin). Its "scalable industrial mid-tier" (silica, maize starch) = rows 3–6 here, real margin but
mostly *reported*, not DPR-verified. Its "value-dense molecular tail" = rows 1–2 & 5, high ₹/kg,
no DPR. **The margin ranking and the savings ranking agree: the bankable money is rice value-add +
RBO + dal-milling IRR; the tail is strategic, not scalable.**

## Caveats

DPR IRR/margin figures are the reports' own projections under stated assumptions (utilisation ramp,
10% price escalation, etc.), not audited outcomes — the Rajasthan RACP set is Grant Thornton
techno-economic modelling; the NIFTEM/NIFTEM-T set is PMFME model DPRs; the NCDC rice-mill anchor is
~2018 vintage. Net margins for milling were computed from the DPRs' own PAT ÷ sales lines. Rows
labelled *reported* draw on private market-research houses (IMARC, NPCS, EntrepreneurIndia) whose
plant economics are indicative, not bankable. Rows labelled *estimated* (nutraceuticals, silica, rice
starch, FRK) have no plant-level DPR and are analyst inference from value-density and gross-margin
data — treat as order-of-magnitude. Vintages span 2018–2027-projection; all URLs and vintages are in
[`data/product_margins_dpr.csv`](../data/product_margins_dpr.csv). Margins are net-on-sales unless the
cell says gross; the IRR column, where present, is the better return signal for the high-turnover
staples.
