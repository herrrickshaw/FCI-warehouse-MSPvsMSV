# The byproduct product tree: what the cooperative sector could make from the crops it already holds

*Value-ranked product map across rice, wheat, maize, pulses, oilseeds and cotton — extending the
byproduct-cascade thesis beyond rice bran oil. Market figures are dated and labelled
verified / reported / unverified; the numbers are the evaluation's inputs, not its conclusions.
Data in [`data/byproduct_products.csv`](../data/byproduct_products.csv). July 2026.*

Cross-references (this report does **not** repeat their arithmetic):

## The thesis in one line

The margin is in the **cascade tail**, not the primary grain. Sugarcane already proves it (sugar
loses money; sugar + ethanol + power + CBG is a business). The grain and fibre book the state holds
has the same latent tail — and at the very top of it sits a cluster of **high-value derivatives India
currently imports or raw-exports while giving the feedstock away**: vital wheat gluten, vitamin E,
high-purity silica, cellulose from cotton linters, specialty starches and phospholipids. The
government already holds the byproduct (bran, husk, germ, linters, gluten-bearing wheat). It captures
the ₹10/kg commodity floor and lets someone else capture the ₹300–15,000/kg tail.

## A. RICE — the deepest tree (the anchor)

Rice is built out elsewhere; here is the full product tail beyond RBO. Mass balance and the RBO/DORB/husk
economics are in [rice_byproduct_massbalance.csv](../data/rice_byproduct_massbalance.csv) and
[rbo-savings-scenarios.md](rbo-savings-scenarios.md) — not repeated.

### From bran (beyond the oil)
- **Gamma oryzanol** *(reported)* — ferulic-acid-ester + sterol nutraceutical, ~1.5% of RBO by weight.
  Global market **USD 2.15 bn (2023) → USD 2.62 bn (2025), ~USD 4.36 bn by 2032**; RBO is ~68% of the
  feedstock. India is the **world's #1 RBO producer (36.7% global share, 2024)** yet its *oryzanol* market
  is only **~USD 6.5 mn** — i.e. India makes the oil and lets others isolate the high-value molecule.
  [grandviewresearch.com](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/gamma-oryzanol-market) (2025).
- **Tocotrienols (TRF) / tocopherols — vitamin E** *(reported)* — rice bran and its deodorizer distillate
  (DD) are a natural vitamin-E source. TRF sells at **USD 150–300/kg**; finished d-alpha tocopherol
  **USD 55–110/kg**. India **imports 60–70% of its natural vitamin E**; domestic tocopherol output is only
  **~150–200 t (30–40% of consumption)**, and the DD feedstock trades at just ₹45–75/kg.
  [industry-experts.com](https://industry-experts.com/verticals/healthcare-and-pharma/natural-source-vitamin-e-tocopherols-and-tocotrienols-an-indian-market-overview) (2026).
- **Rice bran wax** *(reported)* — hard wax recovered in RBO refining; a **carnauba substitute** for
  cosmetics/polish. Unpurified ₹20–35/kg; **purified ₹80–350/kg**. India's potential ~20,000 t vs
  ~10,000 t current unpurified — a value jump captured inside the refinery.
  [cftri.res.in](https://cftri.res.in/technologies/CONP/pwr.pdf) (2024).
- **Ferulic acid** *(reported)* — phenolic antioxidant for cosmetics/pharma; standard ₹450–1,000/kg,
  **cosmetic-grade up to ₹15,550/kg**. Asia-Pacific is 43% of global supply on the back of rice/wheat bran.
  [precedenceresearch.com](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/ferulic-acid-market) (2025).
- **Phytosterols** *(reported)* — cholesterol-lowering plant sterols; part of a **USD 1.1 bn** global market,
  extracted from DD; India largely imports.
  [gminsights.com](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/phytosterols-market) (2025).
- **Rice bran protein** *(reported)* — hypoallergenic; isolate **USD 8–14/kg**, infant-formula pull growing
  15–20%/yr.
  [indexbox.io](https://www.indexbox.io/store/india-organic-rice-protein-market-analysis-forecast-size-trends-and-insights/) (2026).
- **DORB (defatted bran)** *(verified)* — the feed/fibre residue, ₹9–15/kg; export banned to Sep-2025,
  reopened Oct-2025. The commodity floor beneath the whole bran tail.
  [indiamart](https://www.indiamart.com/proddetail/de-oiled-rice-bran-dorb-6146265530.html) (2025).

### From husk — the silica value-jump
- **Precipitated / high-purity silica from rice husk ash** *(reported)* — **the biggest hidden value jump
  in the whole tree.** Raw RHA sells at **₹1–3.5/kg**; converted to **98–99%+ precipitated silica** it feeds
  a global market projected at **~USD 980 mn by 2027**, of which the **tyre industry is ~45%** (rolling-
  resistance filler), with solar and semiconductor grades above that. India produces **>30 mn t of husk/yr**
  and mostly burns or dumps it while importing high-purity silica.
  [pmarketresearch.com](https://pmarketresearch.com/chemi/rice-husk-ash-precipitated-silica-market/) (2027 proj.),
  [iisc CGPL](https://cgpl.iisc.ac.in/oldwebcontent/site/Portals/0/Technologies/PrecipitatedSilica.pdf).
- **Activated carbon / biochar** *(reported)* — husk-derived AC for water treatment; biochar as soil
  conditioner + carbon-removal credits (~USD 177/t CO₂e). RHA market ~USD 2.9 bn global.
  [entrepreneurindia.co](https://www.entrepreneurindia.co/blogs/rice-husk-based-products-business/) (2025).
- **Husk power** *(verified)* — ~0.9 MWh/tonne; already the boiler fuel that can make the whole extraction
  chain self-powered (see biodiesel report). Energy floor, not a molecule.

### From broken rice
- **Rice starch** *(reported)* — gluten-free, hypoallergenic; India market ~**USD 46 mn by 2030** (6.1% CAGR),
  premium over corn starch in pharma/cosmetic use.
  [industryarc](https://www.industryarc.com/Research/India-Rice-Starch-Market-market.html-800712) (2024).
- **Rice protein isolate** *(reported)* — ₹225–450/kg, organic USD 8–14/kg; vegan + infant-formula demand.
  [researchandmarkets](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/6083067/rice-protein-market-product-type-form-source) (2026).
- **Rice FMCG** — poha / murmura / idli-dosa mix / rice flour; the co-op DPR template, branded-staple margin.

### Whole / parboiled grain — the captive-demand product
- **Fortified rice kernels (FRK)** *(verified)* — extruded kernels carrying iron/folic-acid/B12, blended
  **1% (10 g/kg)** into PDS rice. **This is the government's own captive market.** Universal coverage reached
  March 2024 across PDS/ICDS/PM-POSHAN (≈81 crore beneficiaries); **~406 LMT** fortified rice distributed
  2019-20→Mar-2024; program cost **~₹2,700 cr/yr**, **₹17,082 cr allocated to Dec-2028**; installed FRK
  capacity ~111 LMT fortified-rice equivalent. At ~350–400 LMT of NFSA rice, the 1% blend implies a
  **standing ~3.5–4 LMT/yr FRK demand the state itself creates and buys.**
  [lukmaanias](https://blog.lukmaanias.com/2024/10/14/fortified-rice-and-centre-extension-for-its-distribution-in-schemes/) (2024).

## B. WHEAT — the co-anchor (the classic "import the derivative, sit on the grain")

India is the world's #2 wheat producer and the state holds tens of millions of tonnes of it, yet it
**imports the two derivatives that wheat wet-milling produces.**

- **Vital wheat gluten** *(verified import data)* — ~**75% protein**, the key bakery dough-strengthener and
  the base for **meat analogues and pet food**. India **imports ~13,000 t/yr** (2023), **China 88% of supply**,
  at ~**USD 1,292/t** (~₹108/kg). Global vital-wheat-gluten market **~USD 2.59 bn (2025)**. A domestic
  **gluten + starch co-production plant** is the textbook wheat wet-milling play the state could enable —
  captive gluten demand exists (bakery, the growing plant-protein/meat-analogue segment) and the feedstock
  is the wheat it already procures.
  [indexbox](https://www.indexbox.io/store/india-wheat-gluten-market-analysis-forecast-size-trends-and-insights/) (2024),
  [cognitivemarketresearch](https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/vital-wheat-gluten-market-report) (2025).
- **Wheat starch + glucose/maltodextrin** *(reported)* — the co-product of gluten; feeds the **USD 3.45 bn
  India starch-and-derivatives market** (7.3% CAGR), used in food, paper, pharma and ethanol. Roquette-Riddhi
  Siddhi already operates here.
  [mordorintelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/india-starch-and-starch-derivative-market) (2025).
- **Wheat germ oil** *(reported)* — germ is a **wasted milling fraction**; its oil is **the highest natural
  vitamin-E food (255 mg/100 g)** and rich in **octacosanol** (performance nutraceutical). ₹350–600/kg
  domestic, export **USD 5–12/kg**. Tiny volume, very high ₹/kg.
  [tridge](https://dir.tridge.com/prices/wheat-germ-oil/IN) (2024).
- **Wheat bran** *(reported)* — feed / dietary fibre / ferulic-acid feedstock; the commodity floor.
- **Fortified atta for PDS** *(reported)* — the wheat analogue of FRK: iron/folic-acid/B12 atta, ~2.0–2.5
  LMT/yr estimated, currently **voluntary** (ITC/Aashirwaad/Patanjali/Cargill participate). A mandate would
  convert it into captive government demand exactly like FRK.
  [FSSAI](https://fortification.fssai.gov.in/commodity?commodity=fortified-wheat-flour) (2024).
- **Maida / suji / atta** — the large commodity staple base (Bharat-brand route).

## C. MAIZE — the wet-milling product tree

India is the **world's #3 maize-starch producer** (2.6 mn t output, net exporter) but only **~14% of maize is
wet-milled**, and it **imports refined corn derivatives for lack of high-purity processing**.

- **Maize starch** *(reported)* — the wet-milling core; part of a **~₹3,500 cr** organised starch industry.
  [indexbox](https://www.indexbox.io/store/india-maize-corn-starch-market-analysis-forecast-size-trends-and-insights/) (2024).
- **Dextrose / HFCS / sorbitol / maltodextrin** *(reported)* — the sweetener-and-polyol tree; sugar
  substitutes for beverages/dairy/processed food. Gujarat Ambuja and Roquette are building capacity — but
  **India still imports high-purity derivatives**, the import-substitution opening.
  [npcsblog](https://npcsblog.com/maize-processing-industry-in-india/) (2024).
- **Corn gluten meal (60–65% protein)** *(reported)* — premium feed/pigment; India already **exports maize
  gluten to >25 countries** (Gujarat + Punjab ~60% of output).
  [indiamart](https://dir.indiamart.com/impcat/corn-gluten-meal.html) (2024).
- **Corn germ → corn oil + germ meal** *(reported)* — edible-oil byproduct of the germ fraction.
- **Corn gluten feed / DDGS** *(reported)* — the fibre + distillers stream (overlaps the ethanol book).

## D. PULSES — dal margin + besan + the protein trend

- **Besan / gram flour** *(reported)* — a **huge domestic staple**: India produces **>5 mn t besan** (>50% of
  ~10 mn t desi chickpea); global gram-flour market ~USD 2.5–5.6 bn. The state (NAFED/NCCF) procures chana
  and sells it **raw**, capturing none of this.
  [imarcgroup](https://www.imarcgroup.com/india-pulses-market) (2024).
- **Milled dal** *(reported)* — branded-staple milling earns 11–12% EBITDA vs ~0% raw auction (per
  [product-diversification-strategy.md](product-diversification-strategy.md)); the state auctions raw.
- **Pulse / pea protein isolate** *(reported)* — the plant-protein export trend; ₹250–450/kg with **>60%
  conversion margin**, India already exporting to US/UK/EU/UAE. Feed-plant-protein-isolate market USD 85–360 mn.
  [niir.org](https://www.niir.org/blog/technical-and-economic-evaluation-of-pea-protein-isolate-concentrate-production/) (2026).
- **Chuni / husk / brokens** — pulse-milling feed byproduct (commodity).

## E. OILSEEDS — oil + meal + the specialty phospholipid/protein tail

Crush economics and the ₹19.5 bn import-bill logic are in
[product-diversification-strategy.md](product-diversification-strategy.md); the tail here:

- **Edible oil** *(verified context)* — import parity ~₹100–110/kg; India 56–60% import-dependent
  (~₹1.6–1.7 lakh cr bill). The mass import-substitution prize.
- **Oilmeal** *(verified)* — India is a **big exporter**: record **48.86 LMT** in FY24 worth **₹15,368 cr**
  (soybean 21.33 LMT, rapeseed 22.13 LMT). The feed byproduct is already monetised — at commodity value.
  [ibef](https://www.ibef.org/news/india-exports-record-4-8-million-tonnes-of-oil-meal-animal-feed-in-fy24-sea) (2024).
- **Soy lecithin / specialty phospholipids** *(reported)* — feed-grade ₹68–75/kg, but **specialty/pharma
  phospholipids USD 200–350/kg**, which India **imports** while crushing soy domestically. The LNP-drug /
  clinical-nutrition pull is the high-value opening.
  [valuates](https://reports.valuates.com/market-reports/QYRE-Auto-2A16014/global-and-india-soy-lecithin) (2026).
- **Soy protein concentrate/isolate** *(reported)* — India crushes soy but imports concentrates.

## F. COTTON — the seed-and-linter tree (CCI already procures the raw material)

CCI procures cotton under MSP; the ginning byproducts are the tree:

- **Cottonseed oil** *(reported)* — domestic edible oil from CCI-linked seed.
- **Cottonseed meal** *(reported)* — protein feed cake.
- **Cotton linters → cellulose derivatives** *(reported)* — **the raw-export leak.** Linters become
  alpha-cellulose for **viscose rayon, MCC, nitrocellulose and cellulose acetate**; the cellulose-derivative
  market is **USD 1.8 bn (2025) → USD 3.1 bn (2034)**. India **raw-exports first-cut linters to China and
  Bangladesh**, which convert them into the high-purity cellulose and capture the derivative margin.
  [dataintelo](https://dataintelo.com/report/global-cotton-linter-cellulose-market) (2025).

## Evaluation

### 1. Value-density ranking — the hidden high-value tail vs the commodity base

| Tier | ₹/kg band | Products | Where the value hides |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ultra-high (nutraceutical/cosmetic)** | ~₹4,500–25,000/kg | Tocotrienols (₹12,500–25,000), cosmetic ferulic acid (up to ₹15,550), d-alpha tocopherol/vit-E (₹4,500–9,000), phytosterols, high-purity phospholipids (₹16,000–29,000) | Tiny volume, molecule-level extraction, brand/export needed |
| **High (specialty ingredient/protein)** | ~₹200–600/kg | Wheat germ oil (₹350–600), rice/pea/pulse protein isolate (₹225–450), purified rice bran wax (₹80–350), gamma oryzanol | Mid-volume, processing + certification |
| **Mid (starch/derivative/gluten/silica)** | ~₹40–110/kg | Vital wheat gluten (₹108, *imported*), maize/wheat/rice starch, dextrose/HFCS/sorbitol, corn gluten meal, besan, soy lecithin (feed-grade), FRK, fortified atta | Scalable industrial processing; **precipitated silica jumps here from ₹1–3.5 raw RHA** |
| **Commodity base** | ~₹7–40/kg | Flour/maida/atta, DORB feed (₹9–15), oilmeal, pulse husk, raw husk/RHA (₹1–3.5), biochar (₹7), husk power, raw cotton linters | Where the state captures value today — the floor |

**The pattern:** value density rises ~1,000× from the commodity floor (raw husk ₹1–3.5/kg) to the
molecular tail (tocotrienols ₹12,500–25,000/kg). The state, wherever it processes at all, sits at the
bottom two tiers and gifts or raw-exports the feedstock for the top two.

### 2. Mass-scalable vs niche vs aspirational

- **Mass-scalable (volume + real demand):** FRK and fortified atta (captive), maize/wheat/rice **starch and
  its sweeteners**, **vital wheat gluten** (import substitution), **precipitated silica from husk** (industrial,
  tyre-led), besan, oilmeal, edible-oil crush. These can absorb government-scale feedstock.
- **Niche high-value (small volume, high ₹/kg, needs brand/export):** gamma oryzanol, tocotrienols/vitamin E,
  ferulic acid, phytosterols, wheat germ oil, rice bran wax, specialty phospholipids, protein isolates. Real
  money per kg, but the *tonnage* is small — a margin garnish on the cascade, not the load-bearing volume.
- **Aspirational (technically real, commercially unproven at co-op scale):** cotton-linter high-purity
  cellulose / nitrocellulose (India has no domestic converter at scale), semiconductor/solar-grade silica,
  pharma-grade phospholipids for LNP drugs. Right direction, long build.

The honest read: **nutraceuticals are high-value but small-volume; silica and starch are the scalable
industrial plays; FRK/atta/gluten are the volume that fits the state.**

### 3. Which fit the government / co-op model

Two clean buckets:

- **Captive-demand products (the state is both maker and buyer — lowest risk):** **FRK** (₹2,700 cr/yr program,
  already mandated), **fortified atta** (voluntary today; a mandate makes it captive), **fortification inputs**
  (vitamin-A/D/E, iron, folic acid — the state could make the vitamin-E input from its own bran DD instead of
  importing it), edible-oil fortification for PDS/ICDS/MDM. These need *no market* — the demand is the welfare
  programme, and they mirror the demand-security levers in
  [rbo-savings-scenarios.md](rbo-savings-scenarios.md) (contract the demand, don't wait for it).
- **Commercial-market products (need brand/export/industrial offtake):** the nutraceutical tail, protein
  isolates, gluten, silica, cellulose. Best run through **transparent offtake auction** (sell the byproduct
  stream priced-in) or a **ring-fenced commercial arm** (COFCO model), *not* nominated to a private brand — the
  open governance question flagged in the diversification strategy.

### 4. The integrated rice biorefinery — the combined uplift

One rice complex, fed by the paddy the state already directs to CMR millers, running the **full cascade**:

```
PADDY ──► milled rice ──────────────────────────► PDS food grain + FRK (captive)
   │
   ├─ BRAN ─► stabilise ─► RBO ─► refine ─► edible RBO (imports ↓, ~₹8,200 cr — rbo-savings-scenarios.md)
   │                 │                 ├─► rice bran WAX (₹80–350/kg)
   │                 │                 └─► acid oil ─► residual biodiesel (B0.15 ceiling — biodiesel report)
   │                 ├─► gamma ORYZANOL  (nutraceutical, global USD 2.6 bn)
   │                 ├─► TOCOTRIENOLS / vitamin E (USD 150–300/kg; India imports 60–70%)
   │                 ├─► FERULIC ACID / PHYTOSTEROLS (cosmetic ₹450–15,550/kg)
   │                 └─► DORB ─► feed/protein (₹9–15/kg floor)
   │
   ├─ HUSK ─► boiler POWER (self-powers the plant) ─► ash ─► high-purity SILICA (₹1–3.5 → tyre/solar grade)
   │                                                       └─► activated carbon / biochar (+carbon credit)
   │
   └─ BROKENS ─► rice STARCH / rice PROTEIN / poha–murmura FMCG / ethanol
```

**The uplift, qualitatively:** selling raw paddy captures only the MSP (~₹2,369/qtl). The same paddy run through
the full cascade yields (a) the food grain unchanged, **plus** (b) the ~₹8,200 cr edible-oil import saving
(bran → RBO), **plus** (c) the byproduct pool the state currently gifts CMR millers — **~₹20,134 cr gross on
procured paddy, ~₹54,000 cr nationally** (per [omss-processing-returns.md](omss-processing-returns.md) and
[rice_biorefinery_economics.csv](../data/rice_biorefinery_economics.csv)) — **plus** the high-value tail
(oryzanol/vitamin-E/wax/ferulic acid) and the silica value-jump on top. Husk power makes the whole thing
self-financing on energy. The cascade converts a break-even welfare cost centre into a multi-stream
value complex — the sugarcane model, ported to rice.

## The headline: high-value derivatives India imports or raw-exports while holding the feedstock

The single most actionable finding across all six crops — **the state already holds or procures the
byproduct, and India buys back (or ships out raw) the derivative made from it:**

1. **Vital wheat gluten** — India **imports ~13,000 t/yr (~₹140 cr, China 88%)** of a 75%-protein product made
   by wet-milling wheat, while the state sits on tens of millions of tonnes of wheat. A domestic gluten+starch
   plant is both import substitution and a captive-demand (bakery/meat-analogue) play. *[verified import data]*
2. **Vitamin E (tocotrienols/tocopherols)** — India **imports 60–70%** of its natural vitamin E (TRF
   USD 150–300/kg) while the feedstock — rice-bran deodorizer distillate at ₹45–75/kg — is a domestic byproduct
   of the very oil refining the state is being urged to expand. Make the fortification input from the bran
   instead of importing it. *[reported]*
3. **High-purity silica from rice husk** — raw RHA sells at **₹1–3.5/kg**; the **98–99%+ precipitated silica**
   (tyre/solar/semiconductor) it becomes rides a ~USD 980 mn global market — and India, sitting on **>30 mn t of
   husk**, imports the high-purity grade. The single largest value-jump in the tree, and scalable. *[reported]*
4. **Cotton-linter cellulose** — India **raw-exports first-cut linters** to China/Bangladesh, which turn them
   into high-purity cellulose for a **USD 1.8→3.1 bn** viscose/nitrocellulose/MCC market. The state (via CCI)
   procures the cotton; the derivative margin leaves the country. *[reported]*

Runners-up worth flagging: **specialty soy phospholipids** (USD 200–350/kg, imported while crushing soy),
**high-purity corn-starch derivatives** (imported "for lack of high-purity processing infrastructure"), and
**cosmetic-grade ferulic acid** (₹450–15,550/kg from bran India produces in abundance).

## Caveats

Market-size and price figures are labelled *verified* (trade/official — vital-gluten imports, oilmeal exports,
FRK program cost, DORB) or *reported* (private market-research houses and B2B listings — the nutraceutical/
silica/protein prices), and should be read as order-of-magnitude, not audited. IndiaMART/TradeIndia spot
prices span wide ranges by purity and grade. The high-value tail's *volumes* are small: extracting every kg of
oryzanol/tocotrienol from India's bran would still be a niche business — the tail garnishes the cascade, it does
not carry it; the scalable volume is starch, silica, gluten, FRK and the oil/meal base. Import-substitution
"opportunity" assumes the domestic processed product can hit import quality at competitive cost, which for
semiconductor-grade silica and pharma phospholipids is a real technical gap, not a given. The integrated-
biorefinery uplift is a directional sum of separately-sourced streams, not a single audited plant P&L (none
exists at MSV scale). Where this report's streams overlap the cross-referenced reports (RBO import saving,
byproduct giveaway, biodiesel ceiling), those reports carry the primary arithmetic.
